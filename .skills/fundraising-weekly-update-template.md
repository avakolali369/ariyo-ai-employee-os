# Ariyo Fundraising Weekly Update Template

## Core Contract

Ariyo Fundraising Weekly Update Template is not a casual founder note, vanity-metrics email, investor newsletter, or loose progress recap.

It is the governed weekly communication system that turns Ariyo's fundraising motion, investor CRM pipeline, pilot proof, product execution, release readiness, customer evidence, enterprise signals, risks, asks, and next milestones into a concise, credible, evidence-backed update for investors, advisors, warm-intro sources, strategic partners, and internal stakeholders.

Ariyo must not send weekly updates that only create noise.
Ariyo weekly updates must create confidence, urgency, clarity, and next action.

---

## 1. Purpose

This skill defines the structure, tone, evidence requirements, usage rules, and Codex implementation guidance for Ariyo's fundraising weekly update.

The weekly update exists to:

- Maintain investor momentum between meetings.
- Show disciplined execution.
- Convert pilot activity into investor-grade proof.
- Keep warm-intro sources informed without requiring long calls.
- Surface asks clearly.
- Make progress measurable.
- Show risks honestly without weakening confidence.
- Create a record of execution velocity over time.
- Support follow-up inside the Investor CRM Pipeline System.
- Prevent scattered founder updates across email, chat, and notes.

## 3. Audience Types

### 3.1 Investor Prospect

Used for investors who have been contacted but have not yet taken a meeting.

Goal:

- Increase curiosity.
- Show momentum.
- Make a meeting feel timely.

### 3.2 Active Investor Conversation

Used for investors who have taken a meeting or are reviewing materials.

Goal:

- Keep diligence warm.
- Answer objections through evidence.
- Move toward partner meeting, data room access, or term discussion.

### 3.3 Warm Intro Source

Used for advisors, founders, customers, operators, and supporters who can introduce Ariyo to investors.

Goal:

- Make forwarding easy.
- Give them one clear ask.
- Keep them informed enough to advocate.

### 3.4 Internal Founder/Leadership Update

Used internally for fundraising discipline.

Goal:

- Track progress.
- Identify stuck deals.
- Align proof, demo, data room, and outreach.

### 3.5 Strategic Partner or Enterprise Buyer

Used selectively where fundraising and strategic partnership overlap.

Goal:

- Show traction without disclosing sensitive investor pipeline details.
- Reinforce enterprise readiness.

## 4. Weekly Update Principles

### 4.1 Evidence First

Every major claim should be supported by one of:

- Pilot metric.
- Product milestone.
- Customer evidence.
- Investor pipeline movement.
- Release gate progress.
- Marketplace readiness signal.
- Enterprise readiness signal.
- Trust/compliance milestone.

Do not say:

> We are getting strong traction.

Say:

> This week, 3 pilot teams completed onboarding, 2 AI Employees reached production simulation, and one enterprise buyer requested the Trust Center preview.

### 4.2 Short Enough to Read

Default update length:

- 250 to 500 words for external investor updates.
- 500 to 900 words for internal fundraising review.

### 4.3 Same Structure Every Week

Consistency matters more than novelty.
Investors should know where to find:

- What changed.
- What proof improved.
- What is next.
- What Ariyo needs.

### 4.4 Do Not Overshare

External updates must not expose:

- Sensitive customer names unless permitted.
- Exact pipeline terms without consent.
- Security gaps without framing.
- Internal team conflicts.
- Unvalidated revenue claims.
- Private investor names in public-ish updates.

### 4.5 Make One Ask

Each weekly update should include one primary ask.

Examples:

- Intro to enterprise AI/SaaS investors.
- Feedback on enterprise buyer narrative.
- Review of the pilot proof pack.
- Help with security/compliance advisor intro.
- Meeting with a specific partner.

## 5. Required Update Structure

### 5.1 Subject Line

Use one of these patterns:

- `Ariyo Weekly Update — [Key Proof / Milestone]`
- `Ariyo Update — [Pilot / Product / Fundraising Signal]`
- `Ariyo Momentum Note — Week of [Date]`
- `Ariyo Founder Update — [Main Outcome]`

Good examples:

- `Ariyo Weekly Update — Pilot Proof, Dashboard Build, Investor Pipeline`
- `Ariyo Update — AI Employee Builder Demo Now Codex-Ready`
- `Ariyo Momentum Note — First Pilot Readiness Gates Defined`

Avoid:

- `Quick update`
- `Big news`
- `Checking in`
- `Ariyo is moving fast`

## 14. Objection-to-Update Mapping

Weekly updates should respond to investor objections indirectly through proof.

| Objection | Weekly Update Response |
|---|---|
| Is this just a chatbot? | Show AI Employee Builder, workflow, governance, and outcome proof. |
| Is there real demand? | Show pilot activity, customer requests, and enterprise discovery signals. |
| Is it enterprise-ready? | Show Trust Center, admin, audit, SSO/SCIM, compliance, and release gates. |
| Is marketplace too early? | Show marketplace as controlled discovery, not open chaos. |
| Can this scale? | Show API contracts, production readiness audit, and operations command center. |
| What is the wedge? | Show MVP scope lock and specific pilot use cases. |
| What changed this week? | Show exact milestones, not general momentum. |

## 15. Weekly Update Cadence

### 15.1 Fundraising Active Mode

Send weekly.

Use when:

- Ariyo is actively fundraising.
- Meetings are happening.
- Data room is open.
- Pilot proof is emerging.

### 15.2 Warm Relationship Mode

Send every 2 to 4 weeks.

Use when:

- Investor is not actively engaged.
- Advisor is friendly but not in process.
- Market timing is not urgent.

### 15.3 Diligence Mode

Send after meaningful updates only.

Use when:

- Investor is reviewing documents.
- Specific objections are being answered.
- New proof is available.

## 16. Tone Rules

Ariyo weekly updates must sound:

- Clear.
- Evidence-backed.
- Founder-led.
- Enterprise-serious.
- Confident but not inflated.
- Concise.
- Specific.
- Operational.

Avoid:

- Hype.
- Overclaiming.
- Vague traction language.
- Excessive buzzwords.
- Long product explanations.
- Emotional fundraising pressure.
- Unverified growth claims.

## 17. Localization and RTL/LTR Law

### 17.1 Persian and Arabic Updates

When the weekly update is written in Persian/Farsi or Arabic:

- Entire layout must be RTL.
- Text must be right-aligned.
- Tables must support RTL reading order.
- Primary CTA or ask should appear in the RTL-expected position.
- Mixed English terms may remain LTR inside RTL paragraphs where needed.
- Numbers should follow locale formatting decisions consistently.

### 17.2 English and European-Language Updates

When the weekly update is written in English or European LTR languages:

- Entire layout must be LTR.
- Text must be left-aligned.
- Tables must support LTR reading order.
- CTA or ask should follow LTR reading flow.

### 17.3 Codex Rule

Codex must never center-align full investor updates as a substitute for localization.
Alignment must follow language direction.

## 18. Data Room Link Rules

Data room links must only be included when:

- The investor is qualified.
- Permission level is approved.
- Materials are fresh.
- Redactions are complete.
- Access tracking is enabled.

Do not include data room links in broad warm updates.

---

## 19. Confidentiality Rules

Do not include:

- Customer names without permission.
- Investor names without permission.
- Pricing terms under negotiation.
- Sensitive security gaps.
- Private revenue data not approved for sharing.
- Internal team issues.
- Unconfirmed partnership claims.

Use anonymized phrasing:

- `a mid-market operations team`
- `an enterprise buyer in professional services`
- `a regional implementation partner`
- `a security reviewer from a potential enterprise account`

- A claim lacks evidence.
- Customer names are included without shareability status.

## 24. Weekly Update Quality Checklist

Before sending, verify:

- The headline is specific.
- Every major claim has evidence.
- There is one primary ask.
- The update is not too long.
- No internal-only data is exposed.
- Customer names are permitted or anonymized.
- Investor pipeline status is accurate.
- Data room links are permission-safe.
- Risks are framed with owner/action.
- RTL/LTR direction matches language.
- The update creates next action.

---

## 25. Golden Rule

Ariyo weekly fundraising updates must compound trust.

Do not write to impress.
Write to prove progress.
