# Ariyo Executive KPI Scorecard System

## Core Contract

Ariyo Executive KPI Scorecard System is not a generic metrics dashboard, vanity analytics table, investor reporting sheet, or board update appendix.

It is the governed executive scorecard system that defines how Ariyo measures, reviews, explains, governs, and acts on the company’s most important operating signals across product execution, AI Employee performance, Marketplace health, enterprise readiness, customer success, revenue, runway, release readiness, governance, investor confidence, and strategic risk.

Ariyo must not manage the company by activity volume.

Ariyo must manage the company by evidence-backed progress toward a credible, enterprise-ready AI Employee Operating System.

---

## 1. Purpose

This skill defines the KPI scorecard system that leadership, Codex, product teams, operators, customer success, revenue teams, investors, advisors, and governance stakeholders use to understand whether Ariyo is becoming stronger every week.

The scorecard must answer five executive questions:

1. Are we building the right product?
2. Are customers receiving measurable value?
3. Are AI Employees operating safely, reliably, and usefully?
4. Are Marketplace and Enterprise systems becoming credible growth engines?
5. Are we using capital, time, team capacity, and investor trust responsibly?

## 2. Non-Negotiable Scorecard Rules

### 2.1 Evidence Over Opinion

Every KPI must have:

- A named source
- A measurement method
- An owner
- A review cadence
- A confidence level
- A decision implication

No score may be shown without evidence.

### 2.2 Trend Over Snapshot

Ariyo must show:

- Current value
- Previous period value
- Direction
- Target
- Confidence
- Explanation
- Owner action

A single number without trend is not a management signal.

### 2.3 Decision-Oriented Metrics

Every KPI must map to at least one decision:

- Continue
- Improve
- Investigate
- Pause
- Escalate
- Defer
- Launch
- Roll back
- Re-scope
- Fund
- Hire
- Cut

### 2.4 No Vanity Metrics

Forbidden standalone metrics:

- Page views without conversion or intent
- Signups without activation
- AI runs without successful outcomes
- Template views without installs or outcomes
- Investor conversations without next-action quality
- Revenue pipeline without qualification
- Pilot logos without evidence
- Feature count without usage
- Tasks completed without release readiness

### 2.5 KPI Ownership

Every KPI must have one accountable owner, not a committee.

Accepted owner roles:

- Founder / CEO
- Product Lead
- Engineering Lead
- AI Runtime Owner
- Marketplace Owner
- Enterprise Owner
- Customer Success Owner
- Revenue Owner
- Finance Owner
- Trust & Governance Owner
- Operations Owner

## 5. Product Execution KPIs

### 5.1 MVP Scope Completion

Definition:

Percentage of locked MVP surfaces completed according to `mvp-build-scope-lock.md`.

Measured by:

- Surface completion
- Acceptance gates passed
- Critical states implemented
- RTL/LTR support implemented
- Design-system compliance

Decision use:

- Launch readiness
- Sprint planning
- investor update progress
- scope control

Owner:

Product Lead

### 5.2 Mockup-to-Implementation Conversion

Definition:

Percentage of mockup specifications converted into functional frontend surfaces.

Includes:

- Landing Page
- Pricing Page
- App Shell
- Dashboard
- AI Employee Builder
- AI Employee Detail
- Workflow Builder
- Marketplace Homepage
- Template Detail
- Enterprise Admin Preview
- Trust Center Preview
- Launch Readiness Command Center

Decision use:

- Design-to-build discipline
- Codex execution quality
- frontend sprint health

### 5.3 Design System Reuse Rate

Definition:

Percentage of UI built using approved components from `figma-design-system-brief.md` and frontend component contracts.

Good signal:

- High reuse
- fewer one-off components
- consistent layout
- reusable data states

Bad signal:

- New components created per page
- inconsistent states
- duplicated tables/cards/modals

### 5.4 Scope Drift Count

Definition:

Number of features or surfaces introduced outside the locked MVP scope.

Decision trigger:

- Any high-risk drift requires founder/product review
- Repeated drift triggers roadmap governance review

### 5.5 Definition of Done Pass Rate

Definition:

Percentage of completed work units that meet all required gates:

- UI state coverage
- responsive behavior
- RTL/LTR behavior
- accessibility basics
- mock data integration
- analytics event readiness
- error/empty/loading states
- security/privacy boundary

## 6. AI Employee Performance KPIs

### 6.1 AI Employee Activation Rate

Definition:

Percentage of created AI Employees that reach an active, usable state.

Formula:

Active AI Employees / Created AI Employees

Active means:

- Role defined
- outcome defined
- knowledge source connected or simulated
- permissions configured
- workflow assigned or task path defined
- simulation passed
- governance state valid

### 6.2 Successful AI Work Rate

Definition:

Percentage of AI Employee runs that complete with acceptable outcome quality.

Success must consider:

- task completion
- user acceptance
- policy compliance
- no unresolved escalation
- cost within expected range

### 6.3 Human Escalation Rate

Definition:

Percentage of AI Employee work units that require human approval, review, correction, or intervention.

Interpretation:

- Too low may indicate hidden risk
- Too high may indicate poor automation design
- Optimal depends on risk class

### 6.4 Policy Violation Rate

Definition:

Percentage of AI Employee runs blocked, flagged, or escalated due to policy, permission, privacy, or safety constraints.

Must be reviewed by:

- Trust & Governance Owner
- AI Runtime Owner

### 6.5 Cost per Successful Outcome

Definition:

AI runtime cost divided by successful business outcomes.

This is more important than raw AI usage cost.

### 6.6 AI Employee Reliability Score

Composite score using:

- successful run rate
- failed run rate
- latency
- escalation rate
- incident rate
- policy compliance
- cost predictability
- user acceptance

## 17. KPI Health Colors

Use a consistent status model.

### Green

The KPI is on target, evidence-backed, and does not require immediate intervention.

### Yellow

The KPI is acceptable but requires attention, owner action, or trend monitoring.

### Red

The KPI indicates a blocker, material risk, missed target, trust issue, launch issue, or executive decision need.

### Gray

The KPI lacks sufficient evidence or has not been instrumented yet.

Gray is not safe.

Gray means unknown.

## 18. Executive Scorecard Object

Codex and frontend systems should model KPI cards with this structure:

```ts
type KpiStatus = 'green' | 'yellow' | 'red' | 'gray';

type KpiDirection = 'up' | 'down' | 'flat' | 'unknown';

type KpiOwnerRole =
  | 'founder'
  | 'product'
  | 'engineering'
  | 'ai_runtime'
  | 'marketplace'
  | 'enterprise'
  | 'customer_success'
  | 'revenue'
  | 'finance'
  | 'trust_governance'
  | 'operations';

type ExecutiveKpi = {
  id: string;
  name: string;
  category: string;
  currentValue: string | number;
  previousValue?: string | number;
  target?: string | number;
  status: KpiStatus;
  direction: KpiDirection;
  owner: KpiOwnerRole;
  evidenceSource: string;
  confidence: 'high' | 'medium' | 'low';
  decisionImplication: string;
  nextAction?: string;
  lastUpdatedAt: string;
};
```

## 24. Codex Stop Rules

Codex must stop and report if:

- A KPI has no owner.
- A KPI has no source.
- A KPI has no decision implication.
- A KPI conflicts with MVP scope.
- A KPI encourages vanity reporting.
- A KPI cannot be represented in RTL and LTR layouts.
- A metric claim cannot be supported by demo data, mock API, or backend contract.
- A scorecard UI hides red or gray signals.
- A KPI implies financial, legal, or compliance certainty without evidence.

## 25. RTL/LTR Layout Law

Persian/Farsi and Arabic interfaces must be RTL and right-aligned across all KPI cards, charts, tables, filters, summary panels, owner lists, risk registers, decision queues, labels, tooltips, modals, status chips, timelines, exports, and reports.

English and European LTR interfaces must be LTR and left-aligned across all KPI cards, charts, tables, filters, summary panels, owner lists, risk registers, decision queues, labels, tooltips, modals, status chips, timelines, exports, and reports.

Charts must respect direction in surrounding labels, legends, axes, tooltips, and table summaries.

Numeric values may remain numerically formatted according to locale, but alignment and reading order must follow the interface language direction.

If any design instruction conflicts with this rule, the RTL/LTR layout law wins.

## 26. Scorecard Anti-Patterns

Ariyo must avoid:

- Founder vanity dashboards
- investor-facing metrics with no proof
- dashboards that hide bad news
- too many KPIs with no decisions
- one metric per department without system context
- green-heavy dashboards before instrumentation exists
- mixing demo data and real data without labeling
- over-precise numbers from weak evidence
- unowned KPIs
- scorecards that do not trigger action

---

## 27. Acceptance Criteria

This skill is correctly implemented when:

- Each KPI has a category, owner, source, status, confidence, and decision implication.
- Executive, investor, board, product, and production readiness views can reuse the same KPI model.
- Codex can generate typed scorecard data without inventing unsupported claims.
- RTL/LTR is respected across all scorecard surfaces.
- Red and gray signals are visible, not hidden.
- Weekly executive review can be produced from the scorecard.
- Investor updates can be derived without rewriting metrics manually.
- Board governance rhythm can use the same scorecard.
