# Ariyo Marketplace Creator Success System Skill

## 1. Core Contract

Ariyo Marketplace Creator Success is not creator support after publication.

Ariyo Marketplace Creator Success is the operating system that helps trusted creators, partners, agencies, consultants, developers, and internal Ariyo teams build safe, useful, profitable, measurable, and high-quality AI Employee marketplace assets.

Creator success must increase marketplace supply without lowering trust.

Ariyo must help good creators become better, help new creators become safe, help customers discover outcome-producing assets, and prevent low-quality or unsafe creators from scaling.

## 2. Purpose

This skill defines how Ariyo should design, build, govern, and operate a creator success system for the Ariyo Marketplace.

It covers:

- Creator onboarding
- Creator education
- Creator certification
- Creator dashboard
- Creator analytics
- Creator health score
- Template improvement coaching
- Revenue analytics
- Quality coaching
- Marketplace readiness
- Creator lifecycle automation
- Creator support
- Creator trust recovery
- Creator growth loops
- Creator policy education
- Creator success KPIs
- Creator-facing AI assistant rules

The goal is to turn creators into a reliable growth engine for Ariyo without allowing marketplace quality, safety, trust, compliance, or customer outcomes to decline.

## 4. Creator Success Philosophy

Ariyo must treat creators as ecosystem operators, not merely sellers.

A good Ariyo creator does not simply publish a prompt.

A good Ariyo creator packages a repeatable business outcome into a safe, installable, governed, measurable asset.

Ariyo creator success must optimize for:

- Customer outcomes
- Marketplace trust
- Safe adoption
- Quality supply
- Creator revenue
- Lower support burden
- Better template activation
- Fewer incidents
- Sustainable ecosystem growth

Ariyo must not optimize creator success only for:

- Number of creators
- Number of templates
- Raw uploads
- Hype-driven launches
- Short-term marketplace GMV
- Sponsored placement revenue
- Creator vanity metrics

## 5. Creator Types

Ariyo must support multiple creator types with different permissions, risks, training paths, and marketplace privileges.

### 5.1 Internal Ariyo Creator

Creates official Ariyo templates and solution bundles.

Allowed asset types:

- Official AI Employee templates
- Official agent team templates
- Official workflows
- Official onboarding packs
- Official GEO templates
- Official compliance packs
- Official support/sales/operations templates

Requirements:

- Full internal review
- Product owner approval
- Security review for high-risk assets
- Cost estimate
- Evaluation suite
- Versioning
- Public documentation

### 5.2 Certified Consultant Creator

External consultant with approved expertise.

Typical assets:

- Industry playbooks
- Operational workflows
- Customer success templates
- Agency/consulting delivery templates
- Implementation bundles

Requirements:

- Identity verification
- Certification
- Portfolio review
- Policy training
- Creator trust score threshold

### 5.3 Agency Partner Creator

Agency producing templates for clients or marketplace distribution.

Typical assets:

- Marketing automation templates
- Sales/GEO growth templates
- Customer support setups
- Industry-specific bundles
- Multi-client implementation packs

Requirements:

- Partner agreement
- Brand guidelines
- Service quality requirements
- Customer handoff rules
- Revenue share rules

### 5.4 Developer Creator

Technical creator building advanced integrations, API-driven templates, or workflow packs.

Typical assets:

- Integration bundles
- API workflows
- developer tool templates
- internal ops automations
- data sync templates

Requirements:

- Technical validation
- Security review
- OAuth/API permission review
- Webhook safety review
- Rate limit policy

### 5.5 Domain Expert Creator

Expert in a business domain who creates knowledge packs or AI employee behavior guides.

Typical assets:

- Knowledge packs
- Industry guidance packs
- Playbooks
- Review checklists
- Training datasets

Requirements:

- Claims review
- Source quality review
- Accuracy expectations
- Scope disclaimers
- Update cadence

### 5.6 Enterprise Private Creator

Creates templates only for one organization or internal enterprise marketplace.

Typical assets:

- Private workflows
- Internal knowledge packs
- Company-specific AI employee teams
- Custom approval workflows

Requirements:

- Organization-scoped permissions
- Private listing only
- Enterprise admin approval
- Data governance review

## 10. Creator Health Score

Creator Health Score measures whether a creator is becoming more valuable and trustworthy over time.

### 10.1 Score Dimensions

Creator Health Score should include:

- Template quality
- Review pass rate
- Install-to-activation rate
- Customer outcome score
- Rating quality
- Refund/dispute rate
- Incident rate
- Policy violation history
- Creator responsiveness
- Documentation quality
- Simulation coverage
- Update cadence
- Revenue quality
- Support burden
- Trust score trend

### 10.2 Health Score Bands

```text
excellent
healthy
needs_attention
at_risk
restricted
critical
```

### 10.3 Health Score Actions

Excellent:

- Eligible for featured collections
- Faster review for low-risk updates
- Advanced creator opportunities
- Co-marketing eligibility

Healthy:

- Normal marketplace access
- Standard review
- Growth coaching

Needs Attention:

- Improvement recommendations
- Additional education suggestions
- Review depth may increase

At Risk:

- Warning issued
- Publishing limits may apply
- Mandatory coaching or certification refresh

Restricted:

- New publishing disabled for some asset types
- Existing assets monitored
- Payout holds may apply if financial risk exists

Critical:

- Suspension review
- Marketplace Trust & Safety escalation
- Possible delisting or creator suspension

## 14. Creator Success Playbooks

Ariyo must support lifecycle-specific creator success playbooks.

### 14.1 New Creator Activation Playbook

Trigger:

- Creator registered but has no approved template.

Actions:

- Show onboarding checklist
- Recommend first low-risk template type
- Offer sample template structure
- Prompt certification completion
- Provide first submission checklist

Goal:

- First approved template

### 14.2 First Template Improvement Playbook

Trigger:

- First template rejected or changes requested.

Actions:

- Explain review feedback
- Suggest exact corrections
- Link policy sections
- Offer simulation rerun
- Provide examples of approved templates

Goal:

- Resubmission with higher approval probability

### 14.3 Low Activation Template Playbook

Trigger:

- Template installs but users fail activation.

Actions:

- Analyze setup friction
- Recommend install preview changes
- Improve setup instructions
- Reduce required permissions
- Add guided configuration
- Add sample data or demo mode

Goal:

- Improve install-to-activation rate

### 14.4 High Refund Template Playbook

Trigger:

- Refund rate exceeds threshold.

Actions:

- Review listing claims
- Analyze customer feedback
- Check mismatch between promise and behavior
- Adjust pricing or positioning
- Add limitations section
- Consider temporary promotion removal

Goal:

- Reduce refund rate and protect trust

### 14.5 High Incident Template Playbook

Trigger:

- Safety/security/quality incident linked to template.

Actions:

- Pause promotion
- Run incident review
- Require simulation update
- Require creator response
- Potential suspension

Goal:

- Contain risk and prevent recurrence

### 14.6 Creator Growth Playbook

Trigger:

- Creator has high quality and strong outcomes.

Actions:

- Offer featured placement eligibility
- Suggest adjacent templates
- Recommend bundle creation
- Invite to partner program
- Offer co-marketing
- Suggest Smart-tier templates

Goal:

- Expand high-quality creator supply

## 15. Plan-Aware Creator Experience

Creator success must understand Basic, Pro, and Smart marketplace dynamics.

### 15.1 Basic Creator Guidance

Creators targeting Basic customers should build:

- Simple single AI employee templates
- Low-risk knowledge packs
- No complex integrations
- Clear setup
- Low cost
- Strong first value moment
- Minimal permissions

Basic templates must avoid:

- Multi-agent complexity
- Autonomous external actions
- Expensive model dependencies
- Advanced workflow chains
- High setup burden

### 15.2 Pro Creator Guidance

Creators targeting Pro customers should build:

- Operational workflows
- Team templates
- Human review support
- Moderate integrations
- Better analytics
- Department-specific outcomes
- Repeatable business processes

Pro templates should emphasize:

- Reliability
- Integration fit
- Team adoption
- Time saved
- Workflow completion

### 15.3 Smart Creator Guidance

Creators targeting Smart customers should build:

- Multi-agent teams
- Advanced GEO/API templates
- Deep RAG templates
- Complex workflow automation
- Executive dashboards
- Advanced analytics
- Smart routing
- Simulation-heavy templates
- High-value business outcomes

Smart templates require:

- Stronger review
- Advanced simulations
- Cost controls
- Human approval policy
- Detailed documentation
- Incident response readiness

## 21. Security & Privacy Rules

Creator success must protect customer privacy.

Creators may see aggregated performance data.

Creators must not see:

- Customer private content
- Customer internal conversations
- Sensitive logs
- Raw prompts containing customer data
- Customer PII unless explicitly authorized
- Full AI traces unless sanitized
- Organization-specific data without consent

Revenue and install analytics must be privacy-safe.

Support feedback must be redacted when necessary.

## 24. Golden Rule

Ariyo Creator Success must help creators grow only when customer trust grows with them.

Ariyo must never reward creator growth that creates unsafe, low-quality, misleading, costly, or trust-damaging marketplace behavior.

## 25. Final Rule

Every creator success feature must answer:

- Does this help creators build better assets?
- Does this improve customer outcomes?
- Does this protect marketplace trust?
- Does this reduce unsafe or low-quality supply?
- Does this make revenue more durable?
- Does this preserve privacy, security, and governance?

If the answer is not clear, the feature must be redesigned.
