# Ariyo Platform Operating Model Skill

## 1. Core Contract

Ariyo Platform Operating Model is not an org chart.

It is the governed company-and-platform operating system that defines how Ariyo makes decisions, assigns ownership, runs cross-functional rituals, manages product quality, ships safely, supports customers, governs AI Employees, operates the Marketplace, escalates risk, measures outcomes, and scales from founder-led execution into an enterprise-ready operating company.

Ariyo must be operated as a durable AI Employee Operating System company, not as a collection of disconnected product, engineering, sales, support, and AI experiments.

Every major Ariyo motion must have clear ownership, decision rights, operating cadence, evidence, escalation paths, review forums, measurable goals, customer feedback loops, financial discipline, and safety governance.

---

## 2. Purpose

This skill defines the operating model for running Ariyo across product, engineering, AI, marketplace, enterprise, customer success, revenue, finance, legal, support, trust, security, and operations.

It should guide:

- company structure
- product and engineering ownership
- enterprise customer delivery
- Marketplace operations
- AI safety and governance
- revenue operations
- customer success motions
- support operations
- incident response
- planning cadence
- operating reviews
- OKRs and scorecards
- launch decisions
- escalation paths
- cross-functional rituals
- board/investor reporting
- scale readiness

Ariyo must be able to answer at any time:

1. Who owns this problem?
2. Who decides?
3. What evidence is required?
4. What risk level is this?
5. What customer impact exists?
6. What is the operating cadence?
7. What metric proves progress?
8. What happens if it fails?
9. Who is informed?
10. What system records the decision?

## 3. Operating Principles

### 3.1 Customer Trust Over Internal Convenience

Ariyo must not choose operational shortcuts that weaken customer trust, enterprise governance, security, compliance, supportability, or AI safety.

### 3.2 Clear Ownership Over Shared Ambiguity

Every product surface, system, customer motion, marketplace asset class, risk domain, and revenue motion must have a directly responsible owner.

Shared collaboration is encouraged.

Shared ambiguity is not allowed.

### 3.3 Operating Evidence Over Opinion

Major decisions should be based on evidence:

- customer outcomes
- usage data
- support signals
- safety evaluations
- incident history
- revenue quality
- churn/renewal data
- security findings
- compliance evidence
- delivery quality
- market feedback

### 3.4 Governance Without Paralysis

Ariyo must not use governance as a reason to stop shipping.

Ariyo must use governance to ship safely, confidently, and reversibly.

### 3.5 Reversibility By Design

High-risk changes must include rollback, containment, communication, and auditability before launch.

### 3.6 Enterprise Readiness Is Everyone's Job

Enterprise readiness is not owned only by sales or compliance.

It affects product, engineering, design, AI, support, finance, operations, Marketplace, data, and customer success.

### 3.7 AI Employees Require Operating Discipline

AI Employees are not normal SaaS features.

They can act, reason, automate, trigger tools, access data, affect customers, and create business consequences.

Ariyo's operating model must treat AI behavior as an operational risk domain.

## 5. Ownership Model

Ariyo must use named ownership for important assets.

### 5.1 Ownership Types

| Ownership Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Executive Owner | Accountable for the domain outcome |
| Product Owner | Owns customer value and roadmap |
| Engineering Owner | Owns technical delivery and system health |
| AI Owner | Owns AI behavior, evaluations, and model logic |
| Trust Owner | Owns safety, compliance, and policy constraints |
| Revenue Owner | Owns commercial impact and GTM alignment |
| Customer Owner | Owns customer adoption and relationship impact |
| Operations Owner | Owns process execution and reporting |

### 5.2 Required Ownership Records

Ariyo should maintain ownership for:

- each platform module
- each AI Employee category
- each Marketplace system
- each customer segment
- each revenue motion
- each support process
- each trust/safety process
- each critical data store
- each major integration
- each compliance control
- each operating dashboard
- each launch gate

### 5.3 Ownership Anti-Patterns

Ariyo must avoid:

- ownership by Slack channel
- ownership by whoever built it first
- ownership by founder memory
- ownership split across multiple teams with no accountable lead
- unowned customer escalations
- unowned marketplace trust issues
- unowned cost spikes
- unowned AI behavior regressions

## 6. Decision Rights

Ariyo must define who can decide what.

### 6.1 Decision Categories

| Decision Category | Primary Decision Owner | Required Review |
|---|---|---|
| Product roadmap | Product | Engineering, Revenue, Customer Success |
| Architecture | Engineering | AI, Security, Product |
| AI model routing | AI Systems | Security, Finance, Product |
| AI safety policy | Trust/Safety | AI, Product, Legal |
| Marketplace publishing | Marketplace | Trust, AI, Compliance |
| Enterprise launch | Customer Success | Product, Engineering, Security |
| Pricing change | Revenue/Finance | Product, Legal, Customer Success |
| Public claim | Narrative/Marketing | Product, Legal, Trust |
| Compliance posture | Trust/Compliance | Legal, Security, Product |
| Incident severity | Incident Commander | Engineering, Trust, Support |
| Customer escalation | Customer Owner | Product, Support, Executive owner |
| Major vendor approval | Operations/Trust | Security, Finance, Legal |

### 6.2 Decision Rules

A decision must include:

- decision owner
- decision date
- affected systems
- customer impact
- risk level
- evidence reviewed
- alternatives considered
- rollback path
- communication plan
- review date

### 6.3 Disagreement Handling

If domains disagree, Ariyo must escalate through a defined path:

1. Direct owner discussion
2. Cross-functional review
3. Domain executive decision
4. Governance board decision
5. CEO/founder decision for existential issues

Critical trust, safety, security, privacy, and legal risks must not be overridden casually.

## 10. RACI Framework

For major initiatives, Ariyo should use RACI:

- Responsible: does the work
- Accountable: owns the outcome
- Consulted: gives input
- Informed: must be updated

### Example: Launching a Smart AI Employee Template

| Domain | Role |
|---|---|
| Marketplace | Accountable for publishing flow |
| AI Systems | Responsible for AI behavior evaluation |
| Trust/Safety | Accountable for safety approval |
| Engineering | Responsible for runtime and install flow |
| Product | Accountable for customer value |
| Customer Success | Consulted for adoption impact |
| Finance | Consulted for cost/billing impact |
| Support | Informed and prepared |
| Legal/Compliance | Consulted if claims/regulatory risk exists |

### 11.3 Example Company OKRs

- 95% of final readiness gates pass before launch
- Zero unresolved critical security or AI safety blockers
- 10 pilot customers reach activation milestone
