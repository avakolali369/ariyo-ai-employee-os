# Ariyo Sales Demo Playbook Skill

## Purpose

This skill defines how Ariyo should be sold, demonstrated, qualified, priced, piloted, followed up, and converted from lead to paying customer.

Ariyo is not sold as another chatbot.

Ariyo is sold as an AI Employee Operating System: a practical way for businesses to hire, configure, supervise, deploy, measure, and scale AI employees.

This file gives product, sales, marketing, customer success, frontend, backend, and AI-agent contributors a shared operating contract for demos, discovery, objections, pilot offers, pricing conversations, follow-up, and deal progression.

---

## Core Contract

Ariyo sales demos must not show features randomly.

Ariyo demos must prove business outcomes.

Every demo should connect the buyer's pain to a specific AI employee, show how that AI employee is hired and governed, demonstrate a safe first outcome, explain how cost and risk are controlled, and make the next step obvious.

Ariyo must sell confidence, not hype.

## Sales Philosophy

Ariyo must be sold through clarity, proof, and trust.

Do not sell Ariyo by saying:

- "It uses AI."
- "It can answer anything."
- "It replaces your team."
- "It is fully automatic."
- "It is impossible to hack."
- "It will save unlimited time."
- "It can connect to everything."

Sell Ariyo by saying:

- "You can hire AI employees for specific business roles."
- "Each AI employee has a job, knowledge, permissions, workflows, and review rules."
- "You control what the AI employee can see and do."
- "You can start safely with one business use case."
- "You can measure time saved, work completed, cost, quality, and escalation rate."
- "You can choose Basic, Pro, or Smart based on how much capability and automation you need."
- "The product is designed for supervision, not blind automation."

## What The Demo Must Prove

Every serious Ariyo demo must prove five things.

### 1. Ariyo Understands The Business

The demo must show that Ariyo captures business context before acting.

It must answer:

- What does the business do?
- Who are the customers?
- What channels matter?
- What knowledge does the AI employee need?
- What work should the AI employee perform?
- What work is risky and needs review?

### 2. Ariyo Hires AI Employees, Not Generic Bots

The demo must show a concrete employee role, such as:

- AI Customer Support Employee
- AI Sales Assistant
- AI Lead Qualification Employee
- AI Operations Assistant
- AI Knowledge Assistant
- AI E-commerce Assistant
- AI Appointment Assistant

Each employee must have:

- Role
- Goal
- Knowledge
- Channels
- Tools
- Permissions
- Review policy
- Metrics

### 3. Ariyo Produces A Useful First Outcome

The demo must show one realistic outcome:

- A customer answer prepared from knowledge
- A lead qualified and routed
- A support ticket summarized
- A FAQ gap detected
- A workflow started
- A human review request created
- A customer conversation drafted
- A report generated

The first outcome must be understandable in less than two minutes.

### 4. Ariyo Controls Risk

The demo must show:

- Human review for risky actions
- Permission boundaries
- AI cost controls
- Knowledge source limits
- Integration scopes
- Audit trail
- Escalation path

### 5. Ariyo Connects To A Business Decision

The demo must end with a business decision:

- Start a pilot
- Choose a plan
- Connect one knowledge source
- Deploy one AI employee
- Invite an admin
- Schedule implementation
- Review security requirements
- Approve trial scope

## Qualification Framework

Before a full demo, the salesperson or AI sales assistant must qualify the prospect.

### Minimum Qualification Questions

Ask:

1. What kind of work do you want an AI employee to handle first?
2. Which team feels the pain most: support, sales, operations, e-commerce, or management?
3. How is that work handled today?
4. How many people or hours are involved weekly?
5. Which channel matters most: website, WhatsApp, Telegram, email, CRM, helpdesk, or internal knowledge?
6. Do you already have knowledge documents, FAQs, SOPs, product info, or website content?
7. What actions should AI be allowed to do automatically?
8. What actions must require human approval?
9. What would make a pilot successful in 14 to 30 days?
10. Are you evaluating this for Basic, Pro, or Smart-level usage?

### Disqualification Signals

Do not push a full sales process if:

- The buyer wants fully unsupervised high-risk automation immediately.
- The buyer refuses any human review for sensitive actions.
- The buyer expects unlimited AI usage at a fixed low price.
- The buyer has no clear use case.
- The buyer only wants a generic chatbot widget.
- The buyer wants unsafe data scraping or unauthorized automation.
- The buyer asks for functionality that violates compliance, privacy, or security rules.

### Good-Fit Signals

Strong fit exists when:

- The prospect has repetitive customer, sales, support, or operations work.
- The prospect has knowledge sources that can be structured.
- The prospect values measurable outcomes.
- The prospect accepts human review for risky steps.
- The prospect has enough volume to justify automation.
- The prospect wants controlled AI employees, not hype.
- The prospect can identify one first use case.

## Discovery Call Structure

Use this structure for a 20 to 30 minute discovery call.

### 1. Opening

Goal:

- Set context.
- Avoid feature dumping.
- Position Ariyo correctly.

Suggested wording:

> Ariyo is not a generic chatbot builder. It helps businesses hire AI employees for specific roles like support, sales, operations, or knowledge work. The goal today is to understand your first use case and see whether one AI employee can create measurable value safely.

### 2. Current Pain

Ask:

- Where is the most repetitive work happening today?
- What slows your team down?
- What happens when the team is busy or offline?
- What questions or tasks repeat every week?
- What work is important but not handled consistently?

### 3. Current Cost

Ask:

- How many people touch this process?
- How many hours per week does it consume?
- What happens when it is delayed?
- What does poor response time cost you?
- What happens when answers are inconsistent?

### 4. Risk Boundaries

Ask:

- What should AI never do automatically?
- What requires manager approval?
- What data is sensitive?
- Which systems should AI be allowed to read?
- Which systems should AI be allowed to update?

### 5. Success Criteria

Ask:

- What would make this worth paying for?
- What result would prove value in 14 days?
- What result would prove value in 30 days?
- Who must approve the purchase?
- What concerns would block the deal?

### 6. Demo Transition

Say:

> Based on what you shared, I will not show every feature. I will show the shortest path from your use case to one working AI employee, including how it is controlled, reviewed, and measured.

## Demo Structure

Ariyo demos should follow a consistent story.

### Demo Arc

1. Define business context.
2. Choose AI employee role.
3. Add or reference knowledge.
4. Configure permissions and review rules.
5. Connect or simulate a channel.
6. Run a realistic customer/work scenario.
7. Show AI output and evidence.
8. Show human review if risky.
9. Show metrics and cost controls.
10. Recommend Basic, Pro, or Smart.
11. Offer pilot or next step.

## Standard 15-Minute Demo Script

### Minute 0-2: Positioning

Say:

> Ariyo lets a business hire AI employees. Each employee has a role, knowledge, permissions, workflows, cost limits, and review rules. Today I will show one AI employee, not every feature.

### Minute 2-4: Business Context

Show:

- Organization profile
- Industry
- Primary goal
- Main customer type
- Business tone
- Supported channels

Explain:

> Ariyo starts by understanding the business, because an AI employee without business context behaves like a generic assistant.

### Minute 4-6: Hire AI Employee

Show:

- Employee marketplace or role selection
- AI Customer Support Employee, AI Sales Assistant, or AI Operations Assistant
- Role summary
- Capabilities
- Required knowledge
- Required channels

Explain:

> The employee is not just a prompt. It has scope, capabilities, permissions, review rules, and metrics.

### Minute 6-8: Knowledge And Controls

Show:

- Knowledge source selection
- FAQ, website, documents, or manual knowledge
- Permission and review settings
- Safe action boundaries

Explain:

> Ariyo does not ask AI to invent business answers. It grounds the employee in approved knowledge and limits what it can do.

### Minute 8-11: Live Scenario

Show one realistic input:

- Customer asks a support question.
- Lead asks for pricing.
- Operations request needs routing.
- Customer wants appointment or order help.

Show output:

- Answer draft
- Source/citation/evidence where relevant
- Confidence indicator
- Suggested next action
- Human review requirement if needed

Explain:

> This is the first value moment: the AI employee does useful work, but the business remains in control.

### Minute 11-13: Measurement And Cost

Show:

- Tasks completed
- Time saved estimate
- Escalation rate
- AI cost usage
- Plan limit
- Suggested upgrade if relevant

Explain:

> Ariyo tracks whether AI is creating value, not just consuming tokens.

### Minute 13-15: Recommendation And Next Step

Say:

> Based on your use case, I would start with [Basic/Pro/Smart] because [reason]. The safest next step is a scoped pilot with one AI employee, one channel, and one measurable success metric.

## Objection Handling

### Objection: "Is this just a chatbot?"

Answer:

> No. A chatbot mainly responds to messages. Ariyo lets you hire AI employees with roles, knowledge, permissions, workflows, review rules, cost controls, and performance metrics. The goal is not conversation. The goal is business work completed safely.

### Objection: "Can AI make mistakes?"

Answer:

> Yes. That is why Ariyo is designed around controlled AI employees, not blind automation. Risky outputs can require human review, knowledge sources are governed, and actions are permission-based and auditable.

### Objection: "Will this replace my team?"

Answer:

> Ariyo is designed to remove repetitive work and improve response speed. In most businesses, the best result is not replacing people; it is giving the team AI employees that handle routine work while humans handle judgment, relationships, and high-value decisions.

### Objection: "How do we control cost?"

Answer:

> Ariyo tracks cost by organization, AI employee, workflow, model, and plan. Basic has tighter hard limits. Pro gives budgets, alerts, and operational attribution. Smart gives advanced intelligence with predictive controls, ROI tracking, throttling, downgrade policies, and approval gates for high-cost actions.

### Objection: "What if AI says something wrong?"

Answer:

> Ariyo can require review before sending, use approved knowledge sources, show confidence signals, escalate uncertain cases, and log outputs for quality improvement. The goal is supervised AI work, not uncontrolled AI messaging.

### Objection: "Is our data safe?"

Answer:

> Ariyo is designed around organization boundaries, permissions, audit logs, data governance, and integration scopes. Sensitive data access is controlled, and customer data usage must follow the product's privacy and compliance policies.

### Objection: "We already use ChatGPT."

Answer:

> ChatGPT is useful for individual productivity. Ariyo is for business operations. It adds AI employee roles, company knowledge, channels, workflows, permissions, review, analytics, cost controls, and team governance.

### Objection: "We need custom behavior."

Answer:

> Ariyo supports business context, knowledge, employee configuration, workflows, review rules, and integrations. The right question is whether the custom behavior is needed for the first pilot or whether it should come after we prove one measurable use case.

### Objection: "Why should we choose Smart?"

Answer:

> Smart is for customers who want the most capable AI employee experience: stronger reasoning, richer context, more advanced workflows, deeper optimization, and higher-value automation. It costs more because it can use more intelligence, but it also includes stronger controls to protect budget and measure ROI.

### Objection: "Why not start with Basic forever?"

Answer:

> Basic is useful for starting safely. But if AI becomes part of daily operations, businesses usually need Pro or Smart for more knowledge, workflows, integrations, analytics, and control. Basic proves value; Pro operationalizes it; Smart maximizes it.

## Pricing Conversation Rules

Pricing must be tied to outcome and control.

Never say:

- "This is expensive because AI is expensive."
- "Smart is better because it has more tokens."
- "You should buy the highest plan."
- "Unlimited usage is included."

Say:

- "Each plan controls how much AI capability and automation you can safely use."
- "Basic is for validating one use case."
- "Pro is for daily operations."
- "Smart is for advanced AI employees with stronger reasoning, richer context, and deeper automation."
- "Ariyo tracks usage and cost so AI does not become an uncontrolled expense."

## Demo Don'ts

- Overpromise automation.
- Hide limitations.

- Show ungoverned AI actions.
- Ignore cost control.
- Ignore human review.
- Position Ariyo as a chatbot.
- Offer Smart without explaining cost controls.
- Recommend Basic when the use case clearly requires Pro or Smart.
- Recommend Smart only because it is more expensive.

## Deal Qualification Score

Ariyo should score deals from 0 to 100.

### Scoring Inputs

- Clear use case: 20 points
- Repetitive work volume: 15 points
- Existing knowledge sources: 10 points
- Budget alignment: 10 points
- Decision maker access: 10 points
- Timeline urgency: 10 points
- Acceptance of human review: 10 points
- Measurable success metric: 10 points
- Plan fit identified: 5 points

### Score Interpretation

- 80-100: Strong opportunity
- 60-79: Qualified, needs shaping
- 40-59: Early, nurture
- Below 40: Not sales-ready

## Plan Recommendation Logic

### Recommend Basic When

- One simple use case
- Low volume
- Limited budget
- Small knowledge base
- No deep integration need
- Customer wants to test safely

### Recommend Pro When

- Daily operational usage
- Multiple users
- Need review queue
- Need workflows
- Need integrations
- Need cost dashboards
- Need more knowledge

### Recommend Smart When

- High-value use case
- Complex reasoning required
- Larger knowledge context
- Advanced workflows
- Automation depth matters
- ROI justifies higher AI cost
- Customer wants maximum capability
- Customer accepts stronger governance

## Golden Rule

Ariyo sales must make the customer feel three things:

1. "This solves a real business problem."
2. "This is controlled and safe enough to try."
3. "I understand which plan fits me and why."

If a demo does not create those three beliefs, it is not a successful Ariyo demo.
