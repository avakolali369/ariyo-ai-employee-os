# Core Contract

Ariyo customer success is not support.

Ariyo customer success is the operating system that turns AI employee adoption into measurable business outcomes, durable customer trust, retention, expansion, and repeatable growth.

Ariyo must not measure customer success by logins, messages, or AI usage alone.

Ariyo must measure customer success by verified AI employee outcomes: work completed safely, time saved, revenue assisted, support load reduced, knowledge quality improved, workflows adopted, cost controlled, human trust increased, and expansion readiness proven.

Every playbook must connect:

```text
customer goal
→ recommended AI employees
→ required business context
→ required knowledge sources
→ required integrations
→ required workflows
→ human review policy
→ activation path
→ success metrics
→ health signals
→ expansion opportunity
→ risk mitigation
```

# Customer Success Principle

Ariyo succeeds when the customer can confidently say:

```text
We hired AI employees.
They understand our business.
They work inside safe boundaries.
They save measurable time or create measurable value.
Our team trusts them.
We know what they did.
We know what they cost.
We know where to expand next.
```

If the customer cannot connect Ariyo to a business outcome, customer success has failed.

```text

needs workflow automation, measurement, review queues

```

---

# Knowledge Maturity Segments

Knowledge maturity determines AI employee readiness.

```text
Level 0 — Unstructured knowledge:
information lives in people, chats, scattered docs

Level 1 — Basic documented knowledge:
FAQs, policy docs, product pages exist but may be stale

Level 2 — Connected knowledge:
knowledge sources are connected and synced

Level 3 — Governed knowledge:
ownership, freshness, permissions, and review exist

Level 4 — Operational knowledge:
knowledge powers workflows and measurable AI outcomes
```

Customer success must improve knowledge maturity before pushing autonomy.

---

# Automation Maturity Segments

Automation maturity determines safe rollout.

```text
Level 0 — Manual work only
Level 1 — AI drafts only
Level 2 — Human review required
Level 3 — Conditional automation
Level 4 — Autonomous low-risk actions
Level 5 — Governed multi-step automation
```

Do not jump customers from Level 0 to Level 5.

Trust must compound.

# Success Stage Definitions

## Stage 1 — Qualified

Customer has a real pain Ariyo can solve.

Required:

```text
target business function identified
primary pain identified
initial AI employee candidate selected
required knowledge/integration feasibility checked
success outcome defined
```

Not enough:

```text
customer says they want AI
customer requests a demo
customer likes the interface
```

---

## Stage 2 — Activated

Customer reaches first verified value.

Required:

```text
organization setup complete
first employee hired
required knowledge or business context connected
first useful output produced
customer understands review/safety mode
customer sees next action
```

Activation should happen quickly and safely.

---

## Stage 3 — Deployed

Customer has at least one AI employee working in a real channel or workflow.

Required:

```text
employee active
deployment server-confirmed
knowledge readiness confirmed
review policy configured
channel or workflow connected
activity visible
first real business task completed or reviewed
```

Do not mark deployment from UI setup alone.

---

## Stage 4 — Trusted

Customer trusts the AI employee enough to continue using it.

Signals:

```text
review acceptance rate improving
manual edits decreasing
knowledge gaps decreasing
team members return to use employee
customer can explain employee value
few unsafe outputs
clear fallback behavior
```

Trust is measured through behavior, not promises.

---

## Stage 5 — Expanded

Customer adds more employees, workflows, integrations, or higher-value use cases.

Expansion requires:

```text
first value proven
health score positive
usage quality healthy
cost understood
admin confidence high
clear next business function
entitlement or plan path available
```

Do not force expansion before trust.

# First Value Rule

First value must be specific and verifiable.

Good first value moments:

```text
Customer Support Employee drafts an accurate response using connected policies.
Sales Assistant qualifies an inbound lead using business context.
Operations Employee summarizes a request and routes it to the right workflow.
Knowledge Employee identifies missing documentation that blocks automation.
Billing Assistant explains invoice status using safe billing data.
```

Bad first value moments:

```text
user created account
user watched tour
user opened marketplace
user generated generic AI text
user connected a source but no output was verified
```

First value means useful business work happened.

# Customer Goal Model

Every playbook must start with a customer goal.

Goal format:

```text
Business function:
Pain:
Desired outcome:
Current process:
Volume:
Risk level:
Required systems:
Required knowledge:
Human review need:
Success metric:
Time horizon:
```

Example:

```text
Business function: Customer support
Pain: repetitive questions consume team time
Desired outcome: reduce manual replies for common questions
Current process: support team manually replies in email and chat
Volume: 300 tickets per week
Risk level: medium
Required systems: website chat, helpdesk, knowledge base
Required knowledge: FAQ, refund policy, shipping policy
Human review need: required for first 2 weeks
Success metric: 30% reduction in repetitive replies
Time horizon: 30 days
```

Do not start with features.

Start with business work.

# Health Score Categories

Recommended categories:

```text
Healthy:
clear value, safe usage, active adoption, expansion possible

Watch:
value exists but friction or risk signals are present

At Risk:
activation stalled, trust weak, failures high, or value unclear

Critical:
customer may churn, unsafe incident, billing risk, or no adoption
```

Do not hide risk behind numeric scores.

Explain why the score changed.


```text
Data to inspect: readiness checklist, source health, channel connection, permission errors

Success recovery metric: employee deployed and first output reviewed
```

# Customer Success Decision Tree

Ask:

### Has the customer reached first value?

If no, run activation recovery.

### Does the customer trust the AI employee?

If no, inspect review data, knowledge gaps, and safety signals.

### Is knowledge healthy?

If no, run knowledge gap recovery.

### Is the employee deployed and active?

If no, inspect deploy readiness, channel connection, permissions, and owner engagement.

### Is usage creating outcomes?

If no, adjust use case or workflow.

### Is AI cost aligned with value?

If no, run cost concern playbook.

### Is customer healthy and value proven?

If yes, recommend expansion.

### Is renewal approaching?

Prepare evidence-based outcome report.
