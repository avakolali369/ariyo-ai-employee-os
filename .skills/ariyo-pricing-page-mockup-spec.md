# Ariyo Pricing Page Mockup Specification

## Core Contract

Ariyo Pricing Page Mockup Specification is not a generic SaaS pricing table.

It is the governed mockup specification for the public and sales-assisted pricing surface where prospects, customers, founders, operators, enterprise buyers, finance teams, procurement stakeholders, and implementation partners understand how Ariyo packages AI Employees, Marketplace access, governance controls, usage limits, support, trust, and enterprise readiness.

The Pricing Page must make Ariyo feel affordable to start, valuable to grow, controllable at scale, and credible for enterprise procurement.

The page must explain price through outcomes, trust, control, and operating scope — not through arbitrary feature checklists.

Ariyo must never look like another chatbot subscription, automation tool tier table, prompt marketplace, or AI wrapper plan grid.

---

## 1. Purpose of This Mockup Spec

This file defines the design, UX, content, component, state, localization, analytics, and frontend handoff requirements for the Ariyo Pricing Page mockup.

The Pricing Page is a strategic conversion and trust surface. It must do more than show plan names and monthly numbers. It must explain how Ariyo grows from a lightweight AI Employee workspace into an enterprise-governed AI Employee Operating System.

The page must help users answer:

- Which plan is right for my stage?
- What is included in Basic, Pro, Smart, and Enterprise?
- How are AI usage, Marketplace access, governance, support, and integrations controlled?
- What happens when usage grows?
- How does Ariyo prevent unexpected AI cost?
- Which features require enterprise controls?
- Can I safely start small and expand later?
- Is Ariyo credible enough for procurement, compliance, and security review?

The Pricing Page must be designed as a decision support surface, not as a pressure funnel.

---

## 2. Strategic Positioning

The pricing narrative must position Ariyo as:

> A governed AI Employee Operating System with flexible packages for individuals, teams, growing companies, and enterprises.

The page must communicate four ideas clearly:

1. Ariyo is not priced only by chat usage.
2. Ariyo packages AI work capacity, governance, automation depth, Marketplace access, and enterprise controls.
3. Customers can start with a low-risk plan and graduate into more powerful operating tiers.
4. Enterprise buyers can evaluate Ariyo through trust, compliance, support, SSO, audit, and controlled deployment.

## 4. Pricing Architecture

The mockup must support four pricing tracks:

1. Basic
2. Pro
3. Smart
4. Enterprise

### 4.1 Basic

Basic is the low-risk entry plan.

It should feel:

- accessible;
- simple;
- safe;
- cost-controlled;
- suitable for individuals, founders, and early exploration.

Basic should emphasize:

- limited AI Employee creation;
- starter workflows;
- limited Marketplace access;
- simple knowledge upload;
- standard cost controls;
- basic support;
- no complex governance.

Basic must not feel like a toy. It is the safe starting point.

### 4.2 Pro

Pro is the team operating plan.

It should feel:

- collaborative;
- operational;
- powerful enough for daily business work;
- suitable for teams and growing companies.

Pro should emphasize:

- more AI Employees;
- workflow automation;
- team roles;
- approvals;
- integrations;
- advanced knowledge/RAG options;
- Marketplace installs;
- dashboards and analytics;
- improved support.

Pro is the plan most customers should choose.

### 4.3 Smart

Smart is the advanced AI operating plan.

It should feel:

- intelligent;
- automation-rich;
- outcome-focused;
- full-option but governed;
- suitable for serious AI operations before enterprise procurement.

Smart should emphasize:

- advanced AI Employee capabilities;
- simulation;
- memory/context controls;
- advanced workflow orchestration;
- performance analytics;
- advanced Marketplace access;
- stronger governance features;
- advanced cost controls;
- priority support.

Smart must not be framed as simply “more credits.” It is a higher operating capability tier.

### 4.4 Enterprise

Enterprise is the controlled deployment plan.

It should feel:

- governed;
- auditable;
- secure;
- procurement-ready;
- scalable;
- consultative.

Enterprise should emphasize:

- SSO/SCIM;
- enterprise policy engine;
- private catalog;
- data residency controls;
- custom roles and permissions;
- audit logs;
- compliance evidence;
- SLA and support;
- security review;
- procurement and legal flow;
- dedicated success and implementation support.

Enterprise pricing should use “Contact Sales” or “Custom” unless the business explicitly chooses public enterprise numbers.

The pricing page should be designed as a vertically structured conversion page.

Recommended order:

1. Header / Navigation
2. Hero

4. Comparison Table

9. FAQ

The page must support both quick scanning and deeper procurement review.

The header must include:

- Ariyo logo;
- product links;
- Marketplace link;
- Trust Center link;
- Pricing active state;
- “Login” secondary CTA;

- Trust Center must be visible or accessible.

## 10. Price Display Rules

Pricing values may be placeholders during mockup design.

Allowed placeholder patterns:

- “From $X/month”
- “$X / seat / month”
- “Custom”
- “Contact Sales”
- “Usage-based AI cost controls included”

Avoid:

- fake precision;
- unrealistic prices;
- hidden AI cost;
- misleading “unlimited AI” claims;
- claiming enterprise compliance certifications not yet achieved.

## 22. RTL/LTR Layout Law

Persian/Farsi and Arabic interfaces must be RTL and right-aligned across all UI elements.

English and European LTR interfaces must be LTR and left-aligned across all UI elements.

This applies to:

- navigation;
- plan cards;
- billing toggle;
- prices;
- feature lists;
- comparison tables;
- FAQ accordions;
- CTAs;
- badges;
- forms;
- tooltips;
- modals;
- mobile sticky bars;
- footer;
- analytics labels shown in UI;
- legal/trust copy.

### RTL Pricing Rules

For Persian and Arabic:

- page direction must be `rtl`;
- plan cards should align content to the right;
- card ordering may mirror if it improves reading flow;
- numeric prices must remain readable and locale-appropriate;
- currency placement must follow locale rules;
- comparison table row labels should be right-aligned;
- CTA icons should mirror direction where meaningful;
- chevrons, arrows, and progress indicators must be mirrored;
- mixed English product names may remain LTR inside RTL layout when needed.

### LTR Pricing Rules

For English and European LTR languages:

- page direction must be `ltr`;
- all primary elements must be left-aligned;
- plan order should be Basic, Pro, Smart, Enterprise from left to right;
- comparison table labels should be left-aligned;
- CTA icon direction should remain LTR.

## 23. Localization and Internationalization

The pricing page must be localization-ready from the first mockup.

### Required Localization Behavior

- plan names can be localized or kept as branded English depending on market strategy;
- price formatting must support different currencies;
- monthly/yearly terms must localize correctly;
- legal and tax notes must adapt by region;
- Enterprise CTA wording may differ by market;
- long German/French labels must not break card layout;
- Persian/Arabic text must not be forced into LTR components;
- mixed-language templates must be readable.

### Region-Sensitive Elements

- VAT/tax notes;
- currency;
- procurement messaging;
- data residency statements;
- compliance evidence availability;
- enterprise sales contact method.

---

## 24. Plan-Aware States

### Not Selected State

Default view with no user plan selected.

### Selected Plan State

When a user clicks a plan, the page may reveal:

- plan summary;
- next steps;
- selected plan CTA;
- plan-specific FAQ;
- required account creation step.

### Existing Customer State

If logged in:

- show current plan;
- show upgrade/downgrade options;
- show usage limits;
- show billing admin restriction if user cannot change plan.

### Enterprise Workspace State

If workspace is Enterprise:

- show “Contact admin” or “Managed by contract”;
- hide self-serve upgrade actions;
- show procurement/contact links.

---

The mockup must define:

- pricing unavailable state;

- plan deprecated state;

- tax estimate unavailable state;
- user lacks billing permission;
- enterprise contract-managed billing;

Required:

- semantic table structure where table is used;
- accessible accordions;
- visible focus states;
- sufficient contrast;
- clear price labels;
- no critical information conveyed only by color;
- understandable CTA labels;
- reduced motion support.

## 28. Security, Privacy, and Compliance

The pricing page itself is public, but it still affects trust.

Rules:

- do not expose internal pricing logic;
- do not show customer-specific billing data unless authenticated;
- do not show enterprise contract information publicly;
- do not make unsupported compliance claims;
- do not imply unrestricted data access;
- link to Trust Center for evidence instead of overloading pricing page.

## 33. Forbidden Pricing Patterns

Do not design Ariyo Pricing as:

- a cheap chatbot subscription page;
- a credit-only AI pricing table;
- a cluttered enterprise feature spreadsheet;
- a fake “unlimited AI” offer;
- an aggressive countdown/urgency sales page;
- a hidden-fee pricing page;
- a plan comparison that makes governance look optional when it is required;
- a consumer app pricing page;
- a prompt marketplace pricing grid;
- a pricing surface that ignores AI cost control.

---

## 34. Acceptance Criteria

The mockup is acceptable when:

- a founder can pick a starting plan confidently;
- a team manager understands why Pro exists;
- an advanced operator understands Smart;
- an enterprise buyer understands why Enterprise is different;
- AI cost controls are visible;
- Marketplace access is clearly explained;
- governance and trust are not buried;
- no unsupported compliance claims appear;
- RTL/LTR behavior is explicitly represented;
- mobile pricing comparison remains usable;
- the design feels like Ariyo, not a generic SaaS pricing page.

---

## 35. Golden Rule

Ariyo Pricing must sell controlled AI operating capacity, not software features alone.

The user should leave the page understanding what they can safely start with, what they can grow into, and why Ariyo becomes more valuable as AI Employees become part of daily operations.
