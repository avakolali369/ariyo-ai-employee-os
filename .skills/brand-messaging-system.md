# Ariyo Brand Messaging System Skill

## Purpose

This skill defines Ariyo's official brand messaging system.

Use this skill whenever designing, writing, reviewing, or implementing Ariyo messaging across the product, landing pages, pricing pages, sales assets, investor materials, onboarding flows, email campaigns, ads, help content, in-product empty states, upgrade prompts, marketplace pages, enterprise trust pages, and customer-facing communications.

Ariyo must communicate with the clarity of an operating system, the trust of enterprise software, the confidence of a premium SaaS product, and the practicality of a tool that helps real businesses get work done.

Ariyo must not sound like a chatbot builder, AI novelty, prompt playground, generic automation app, or hype-driven AI wrapper.

# Messaging Pillars

All Ariyo messaging should map to at least one of these pillars.

## 1. Hire AI Employees

Ariyo should make AI feel operational, not abstract.

Say:

- Hire an AI employee
- Choose the role your business needs
- Deploy a support, sales, or operations AI employee
- Add AI labor to your team
- Give every team an AI teammate with rules and supervision

Avoid:

- Create a bot
- Build an agent
- Generate a chatbot
- Play with AI
- Automate everything magically

## 2. Train With Business Context

Ariyo must emphasize that useful AI depends on business-specific context.

Say:

- Give Ariyo your business context
- Connect trusted knowledge
- Teach your AI employee how your business works
- Keep answers grounded in your real documents and policies
- Let AI work from your actual business knowledge

Avoid:

- AI knows everything
- No setup required for all businesses
- Just ask anything
- Upload everything without structure

## 3. Supervise and Govern

Ariyo must make human oversight feel like a strength.

Say:

- Review sensitive actions before they happen
- Keep humans in control
- Set approval rules
- Decide what AI can do automatically
- Audit decisions and actions

Avoid:

- Fully autonomous without limits
- Remove humans entirely
- AI makes all decisions
- Set it and forget it for critical work

## 4. Connect Workflows and Tools

Ariyo should frame integrations as capability bridges.

Say:

- Connect the tools your team already uses
- Let AI employees work across channels and systems
- Sync knowledge, receive events, and trigger governed actions
- Connect support, sales, operations, knowledge, and billing workflows

Avoid:

- Connect everything blindly
- Unlimited integrations with no scope
- Raw API access without permissions

## 5. Measure Outcomes

Ariyo must sell outcomes, not usage.

Say:

- Track work completed
- Measure time saved
- See support load reduced
- Attribute outcomes to AI employees
- Understand cost per employee and workflow
- Prove ROI

Avoid:

- More chats
- More tokens
- More AI usage
- Engagement as the only metric

## 6. Control Cost and Risk

Ariyo should make customers feel safe adopting AI.

Say:

- Set budgets
- Control usage by plan, employee, workflow, and organization
- Avoid runaway AI costs
- Use stronger intelligence when it creates value
- Upgrade when more capability is needed

Avoid:

- Unlimited AI without explanation
- Hidden cost
- Surprise bills
- Smart means uncontrolled

# Plan Messaging: Basic, Pro, Smart

Ariyo must communicate Basic, Pro, and Smart clearly. Plan names are not labels only; they represent distinct levels of capability, intelligence, control, and cost.

## Plan Positioning Summary

| Plan | Customer Meaning | Best For | Brand Message |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basic | Start safely with a simple AI employee | Small teams and first-time AI adoption | A safe first AI employee for essential business help |
| Pro | Run AI employees operationally | Growing businesses and teams | AI employees for daily business workflows |
| Smart | Full-power AI employee operations | Advanced teams, automation-heavy businesses, premium users | The most intelligent and capable Ariyo experience |

## Basic Messaging

### Positioning

Basic is the safe, simple, lower-cost starting point.

### Message

> Basic gives you a useful AI employee for essential business work with simple controls and predictable limits.

### What Basic Should Communicate

- Start quickly
- Lower risk
- Lower cost
- Simple setup
- Essential AI employee capabilities
- Limited automation
- Limited integrations
- Basic knowledge grounding
- Basic usage controls

### What Basic Must Not Promise

- Full automation
- Advanced reasoning
- Unlimited knowledge
- Multi-channel complexity
- Deep workflow orchestration
- Enterprise governance
- Smart-level intelligence

### Example Upgrade Prompt

> You are using Basic. To unlock more AI employees, stronger intelligence, advanced workflows, deeper knowledge, and better cost controls, upgrade to Pro or Smart.

## Pro Messaging

### Positioning

Pro is the operational plan for businesses using AI employees in daily work.

### Message

> Pro gives your team AI employees that can support daily workflows, connect more knowledge, use more integrations, and provide better visibility into cost and outcomes.

### What Pro Should Communicate

- More operational capacity
- More workflows
- More integrations
- Better review controls
- More AI employee roles
- Better analytics
- Per-employee and per-workflow cost tracking
- Practical business automation
- Stronger support/sales/operations use cases

### What Pro Must Not Promise

- Unlimited cost
- Smart-level automation
- Full enterprise-grade controls unless included
- Fully autonomous critical decisions

### Example Upgrade Prompt

> Pro unlocks more operational AI employee capacity, advanced workflows, stronger business context, and better cost visibility for growing teams.

## Smart Messaging

### Positioning

Smart is the fullest, most intelligent, most capable Ariyo experience.

### Message

> Smart gives your business Ariyo's most capable AI employees, deeper reasoning, richer knowledge use, advanced automation, premium controls, and intelligent cost governance.

### What Smart Should Communicate

- Full-featured AI employee operations
- Stronger model access
- Advanced reasoning
- Deeper RAG and knowledge workflows
- More automation
- Advanced integrations
- Predictive budget controls
- Advanced analytics
- Smart routing and fallback
- Approval gates for high-cost actions
- ROI tracking
- Enterprise-style governance where applicable

### What Smart Must Not Promise

- Unlimited AI without governance
- No human review for risky actions
- Guaranteed perfect answers
- No security or compliance limits
- No cost boundaries

### Smart Safety Message

> Smart gives you the most powerful Ariyo experience, but intelligence still runs inside budgets, permissions, review gates, safety checks, and audit logs.

Error copy must be calm, actionable, and non-technical unless needed.

### AI Execution Error

> This AI employee could not complete the task. No action was taken. You can retry, review the setup, or contact support if the issue continues.

### Permission Error

> You do not have permission to perform this action. Ask an organization admin to update your access if needed.

### Budget Limit Error

> This action was paused because it may exceed your current AI budget. Review your usage or upgrade your plan to continue.

# Objection Handling Messaging

## "Is this just a chatbot?"

Response:

> No. Ariyo can include chat experiences, but the product is broader. Ariyo lets you hire AI employees, connect knowledge, assign workflows, set permissions, review sensitive actions, integrate tools, and measure outcomes.

## "Can AI make mistakes?"

Response:

> Yes. Ariyo is designed with that reality in mind. It uses trusted knowledge, review gates, permissions, evaluation, audit logs, and escalation rules to reduce risk and keep humans in control.

## "Will this replace my team?"

Response:

> Ariyo is designed to handle repetitive work, support teams, and create leverage. It works best when humans define goals, supervise sensitive work, and use AI employees to increase capacity.

## "How do we control cost?"

Response:

> Ariyo tracks usage by plan, organization, employee, workflow, and model. Teams can set budgets, limits, alerts, throttling, caching, downgrade policies, and approval gates for high-cost actions.

## "Is it secure?"

Response:

> Ariyo is designed around organization boundaries, permissions, scoped integrations, audit logs, data governance, security review gates, and threat modeling. Security claims must always match implemented controls and available evidence.

# In-Product Microcopy Rules

## Buttons

Buttons should be action-oriented.

Good:

- Hire AI employee
- Connect knowledge
- Create workflow
- Review action
- Deploy safely
- Upgrade to Smart

Avoid:

- Submit
- Click here
- Do it
- Generate
- Configure thing

## Tooltips

Tooltips should explain why a thing matters, not restate the label.

Bad:

> Budget controls your budget.

Good:

> Set a monthly AI spend limit so this employee cannot create unexpected costs.

## Review Prompts

Review prompts must explain risk.

Example:

> This action may send a message to a customer. Review the content before Ariyo sends it.

# Trust and Compliance Messaging Rules

## Legal Disclaimer Style

Ariyo may state product behavior, but must not provide legal conclusions unless approved.

Good:

> Ariyo supports data export and deletion workflows.

Risky:

> Ariyo makes you fully compliant with every privacy regulation.

Good:

> Ariyo includes tools that can support governance, auditability, and privacy workflows.

Risky:

> Ariyo guarantees compliance.

## AI Disclosure Messaging

When needed:

> This response was generated by an AI employee and may require human review depending on your organization's settings.

## Sensitive Decision Messaging

> Ariyo should not make high-impact decisions without human review and clearly configured authority.

# Brand Messaging QA Checklist

Before shipping any customer-facing copy, verify:

- The copy clearly says Ariyo is an AI Employee Operating System.
- It avoids chatbot-builder framing unless specifically comparing.
- It communicates business outcome.
- It does not overpromise autonomy, security, compliance, or accuracy.
- It explains trust controls when relevant.
- It uses Basic/Pro/Smart correctly.
- It uses customer language, not internal architecture labels.
- It has one primary CTA where possible.
- It supports mobile readability.
- It sounds premium and restrained.
- It does not create legal or security risk.
