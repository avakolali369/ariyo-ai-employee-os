# Ariyo Core Product Mockup Brief

## Core Contract

Ariyo Core Product Mockup Brief is not a request to make the product look beautiful.

It is the governed design brief that translates Ariyo's Master Skill Index and Product Surface Map into the first high-confidence product mockups that can communicate Ariyo's category, product value, enterprise seriousness, marketplace depth, AI Employee operating model, trust posture, and launch-readiness to customers, investors, designers, engineers, and internal operators.

Ariyo mockups must make the product understandable before they make it impressive.

Ariyo mockups must show what the platform does, who it serves, why it is trusted, how work gets done, how AI Employees are governed, how Marketplace assets are discovered, and how enterprise teams stay in control.

## 3. Mockup Philosophy

Ariyo's first mockups must not feel like:

- a generic AI chatbot app
- a simple prompt library
- a lightweight automation tool
- a marketplace of random templates
- a project management dashboard
- a customer support bot
- a low-trust AI experiment

Ariyo's first mockups must feel like:

- an AI Employee Operating System
- a governed enterprise AI work platform
- a trusted marketplace for AI Employee assets
- a system for building, deploying, monitoring, and improving AI workers
- a platform that combines productivity, governance, safety, measurement, and scale

- Governance
- Workflow

- Simulation
- Approval

- Evidence
- Control
- Readiness

- magic
- unlimited

## 29. Product Risks to Avoid in Mockup

- Designing too many screens before validating core narrative
- Making Marketplace look like a template store without governance
- Making AI Employee Builder look like a prompt form
- Hiding enterprise controls too deep
- Making dashboards look data-rich but decision-poor
- Using fake metrics without explaining what they represent
- Making Smart plan feel like unlimited AI usage
- Ignoring trust labels and evidence
- Ignoring approval and rollback states
- Ignoring localization and RTL needs

---

## 30. Success Criteria

The mockup is successful if a viewer can answer:

1. What is Ariyo?
2. Who is it for?
3. Why is it not just another chatbot?
4. How do I create an AI Employee?
5. How do I govern AI Employees?
6. How does the Marketplace work?
7. Why should I trust Marketplace templates?
8. What makes Basic, Pro, and Smart different?
9. How does Ariyo prove business value?
10. How would an enterprise control risk?
11. What would the MVP include?
12. What would an investor remember after the demo?

---

## 31. Golden Rule

Ariyo mockups must make the operating system visible.

Do not design isolated pages.

Design the evidence that Ariyo is a serious, governed, scalable AI Employee Operating System.
