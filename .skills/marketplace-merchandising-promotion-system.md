# Ariyo Marketplace Merchandising & Promotion System Skill

## Core Contract

Ariyo Marketplace Merchandising & Promotion System is not a banner manager.

It is the governed commercial discovery system that decides how Marketplace assets, AI Employee templates, Agent Team templates, Workflow templates, Knowledge packs, GEO templates, Integration bundles, certified templates, partner solutions, internal collections, sponsored placements, seasonal campaigns, and editorial recommendations are packaged, promoted, explained, measured, and controlled.

Ariyo must promote useful, safe, trusted, relevant, and outcome-producing Marketplace assets.

Ariyo must never let paid promotion, internal preference, creator influence, short-term revenue, or visual prominence override customer safety, trust labels, certification truth, quality ranking, compliance constraints, plan fit, region compatibility, or customer intent.

## 3. Merchandising Philosophy

Ariyo Marketplace merchandising must follow these principles:

1. Trust before revenue.
2. Customer fit before creator preference.
3. Certified truth before marketing claims.
4. Quality signals before paid placement.
5. Outcome evidence before popularity.
6. Plan compatibility before visual prominence.
7. Region compatibility before install suggestions.
8. Safety and compliance before conversion.
9. Explainability before persuasion.
10. Long-term Marketplace health before short-term campaign metrics.

Merchandising must help customers discover the right asset faster.

Merchandising must not trick customers into installing the wrong asset faster.

- No active severe incidents.
- Acceptable health score.
- Accurate listing claims.
- Valid trust labels.
- Strong review or outcome signals, unless new launch exception applies.

- Relevance checks.

## 6. Promotion Eligibility Model

Every promotion candidate must pass eligibility checks before being shown.

### 6.1 Required Eligibility Checks

A template may be promoted only if:

- Asset status is approved or eligible for controlled launch.
- No active severe policy enforcement state exists.
- No unresolved critical incident exists.
- No unresolved critical dependency failure exists.
- Trust labels are current.
- Region compatibility is known.
- Plan compatibility is known.
- Pricing and billing terms are clear.
- Claims are reviewed and not misleading.
- Creator/vendor status is not suspended.
- Review fraud signals are below threshold.
- Ranking manipulation signals are below threshold.
- Installation entitlement checks can be completed.
- Required evidence is not expired for enterprise surfaces.

### 6.2 Optional Positive Signals

Positive promotion signals include:

- Certification level.
- High activation rate.
- Strong outcome evidence.
- Low support burden.
- Low refund rate.
- Low unsafe output rate.
- High creator response rate.
- Strong customer reviews.
- Strong enterprise readiness score.
- Recent quality improvement.
- Strong documentation completeness.
- Accurate setup time.
- Cost predictability.
- Low incident history.

### 6.3 Hard Blocks

A template must not be promoted if:

- It is suspended.
- It is region-blocked for the viewer.
- It is plan-incompatible and no upgrade path is allowed.
- It has unresolved critical safety findings.
- It has active malware, data exfiltration, or malicious automation risk.
- It violates Marketplace policy.
- It has expired required legal/security evidence on enterprise surfaces.
- Creator/vendor is suspended or payout-blocked for trust reasons.
- Sponsored campaign attempts to hide its commercial nature.

## 10. Sponsored Placement Governance

Sponsored placements are allowed only when they improve discovery without corrupting trust.

### 10.1 Sponsored Placement Requirements

Every sponsored placement must:

- Be clearly labeled as sponsored.
- Show why it is relevant.
- Pass safety and policy checks.
- Pass install compatibility checks.
- Respect private catalog rules.
- Respect region controls.
- Respect customer opt-out where applicable.
- Never hide trust warnings.
- Never hide certification gaps.
- Never hide creator/vendor restrictions.
- Never replace required security/compliance warnings.

### 10.2 Sponsored Ranking Limitations

Sponsored placement must not:

- Promote suspended assets.
- Promote assets with unresolved critical incidents.
- Promote assets with manipulated reviews.
- Override private catalog blocklists.
- Override procurement controls.
- Override region-blocked restrictions.
- Override customer plan restrictions.
- Appear as organic ranking.
- Use certification-like visuals unless certified.

### 10.3 Sponsored Placement Label

Customer-facing labels must be clear:

- "Sponsored"
- "Promoted by creator"
- "Partner promotion"
- "Ariyo editorial pick"
- "Featured by your admin"

Never use vague labels like:

- "Recommended" when paid.
- "Top pick" when paid.
- "Trusted" when merely sponsored.
- "Certified" when not certified.

## 11. Anti-Pay-to-Win Rules

Ariyo Marketplace must prevent paid promotion from dominating organic discovery.

Required protections:

- Sponsored inventory caps per page.
- Quality-weighted sponsorship eligibility.
- Relevance minimum threshold.
- Trust minimum threshold.
- Review fraud screening.
- Creator trust screening.
- Spend velocity limits for new creators.
- Category fairness limits.
- Enterprise opt-out support.
- Private catalog override controls.
- Campaign audit trail.

Paid promotion can buy attention.

Paid promotion cannot buy trust.

Paid promotion cannot buy certification.

Paid promotion cannot bypass safety.

## 14. Plan-Aware Promotion Rules

### 14.1 Basic Plan

Basic promotion must prioritize:

- Low-risk templates.
- Simple setup.
- Low cost.
- Clear value.
- Limited permissions.
- Fast time-to-first-value.
- Safe defaults.
- No complex multi-agent dependencies.

Do not promote:

- High-cost templates.
- Complex enterprise workflows.
- High-permission automations.
- Templates requiring many integrations.
- Advanced Smart-only features without upgrade clarity.

### 14.2 Pro Plan

Pro promotion must prioritize:

- Team workflows.
- Operational automation.
- CRM and customer lifecycle templates.
- Department-level templates.
- Moderate integrations.
- Strong reliability.
- Clear ROI.
- Creator or partner-supported templates.

### 14.3 Smart Plan

Smart promotion may include:

- Multi-agent templates.
- Advanced GEO systems.
- RAG-heavy workflows.
- Enterprise integrations.
- Automated decision support.
- Cross-department workflows.
- Advanced analytics templates.

But Smart promotion must also show:

- Cost forecast.
- Permission scope.

- Safety posture.
- Human review requirements.
- Region constraints.

## 15. Promotion Ranking Formula

Ariyo may use a hybrid formula:

```txt
promotion_score =
  relevance_score * 0.25
+ quality_score * 0.20
+ trust_score * 0.20
+ outcome_score * 0.15
+ plan_fit_score * 0.10
+ campaign_fit_score * 0.05
+ freshness_score * 0.05
- risk_penalty
- support_burden_penalty
- compliance_gap_penalty
- review_integrity_penalty
```

Sponsored promotion may add a commercial boost only after all eligibility thresholds are met.

```txt
final_promoted_score = promotion_score + allowed_sponsored_boost
```

The sponsored boost must be capped.

The boost must not override hard safety or compliance blocks.

## 29. Security and Privacy Rules

Promotion systems must:

- Respect role-based access.
- Avoid exposing private catalog data publicly.
- Avoid leaking enterprise adoption data.
- Avoid using sensitive personal data for targeting.
- Avoid revealing procurement status to unauthorized users.
- Log campaign changes.
- Protect advertiser billing data.
- Protect creator performance data.
- Redact customer-specific campaign analytics where needed.

## 30. KPI Framework

### 30.1 Discovery Quality KPIs

- Qualified promotion click-through rate.
- Promotion install-to-activation rate.
- Promotion outcome completion rate.
- Promotion retention rate.

## 31. Forbidden Patterns

Ariyo must never:

- Show sponsored content without disclosure.
- Promote suspended templates.
- Promote region-blocked templates.
- Promote templates with unresolved critical incidents.
- Use certification visuals for uncertified assets.
- Hide trust warnings inside promoted placements.
- Let paid placements override private catalog policy.
- Let creator spend override customer fit.
- Optimize merchandising only for clicks.
- Use fake scarcity or fake urgency.
- Promote assets with misleading claims.
- Promote high-risk Smart-only templates to Basic users without clear upgrade and governance explanation.
- Hide pricing, setup complexity, or permissions.
- Promote templates with expired enterprise evidence on enterprise surfaces.
- Allow campaign analytics to expose customer-sensitive information.

---

## 32. Done Criteria

This skill is done when:

- Marketplace has governed campaigns and collections.
- Sponsored placements are clearly labeled.
- Promotion eligibility checks exist.
- Trust and safety blocks override promotion.
- Plan and region-aware promotion works.
- Private catalog merchandising works.
- Campaign guardrails can pause risky campaigns.
- Campaign analytics measure outcomes and risk.
- Creator and partner promotion workflows are governed.
- Paid promotion cannot buy trust.

---

## 33. Golden Rule

Ariyo Marketplace merchandising must increase the visibility of customer value, not the visibility of whoever can shout, pay, or manipulate the loudest.
