# Ariyo Fundraising Close Plan

## Core Contract

Ariyo Fundraising Close Plan is not a generic closing checklist, legal task tracker, founder memory aid, or investor follow-up note.

It is the governed closing operating system that tells Ariyo how to move from qualified investor interest and term readiness to committed capital, diligence completion, legal execution, investor communications, closing coordination, and post-close operational handoff.

Ariyo must not treat a verbal yes as a closed round.

Ariyo closes only when commitments, diligence, documents, funds flow, investor records, cap table updates, legal sign-off, communication plan, and post-close operating responsibilities are complete.

---

## 1. Purpose

This skill defines the disciplined fundraising close process for Ariyo after the company has already prepared:

- Investor narrative
- Pilot proof
- Investor CRM pipeline
- Data room
- Weekly update system
- Terms readiness checklist
- Diligence materials
- Use-of-funds logic
- Investor objection handling
- Legal readiness inputs

The goal is to prevent fundraising momentum from decaying between investor interest and actual close.

This file gives Codex, founders, advisors, finance operators, counsel, and investor relations operators a governed sequence for managing the closing process.

---

## 2. Non-Legal Notice

This skill is not legal, tax, securities, accounting, valuation, or investment advice.

Ariyo must involve qualified counsel and finance advisors before issuing investment documents, negotiating terms, accepting funds, changing the cap table, or making investor representations.

Codex must not generate legally binding investment documents as final authority. Codex may help organize work, create drafts, track readiness, summarize status, and surface gaps, but counsel-approved documents must control the close.

## 4. Closing Philosophy

Ariyo fundraising close is governed by five principles:

1. **Evidence before urgency**  
   Urgency must come from traction, proof, scarcity, and readiness, not pressure language.

2. **Commitment before celebration**  
   Investor interest is not capital. A meeting, verbal support, or positive email is not a close.

3. **Documents before funds**  
   Funds must not be accepted casually without correct documentation, authorization, compliance, and counsel sign-off.

4. **Clarity before negotiation**  
   Ariyo must know what it can accept, what it cannot accept, and what must be escalated.

5. **Post-close accountability before announcement**  
   Closing capital creates obligations: communication, reporting, execution, runway discipline, and governance.

## 5. Fundraising Close Stages

Ariyo must manage close through the following stages:

### Stage 0 — Close Readiness Confirmed

Inputs:

- Terms readiness checklist passed
- Data room complete enough for active diligence
- Lead investor target identified
- Investor pipeline segmented
- Round size logic approved internally
- Use-of-funds plan ready
- Counsel identified
- Founder decision rules defined

Output:

- Closing process may begin

### Stage 1 — Lead Investor Process

Goal:

Identify, qualify, and move the strongest investor toward lead-level commitment.

Required fields:

- Investor name
- Partner/contact
- Fit score
- Strategic relevance
- Check size potential
- Lead likelihood
- Decision process
- Required diligence
- Key objections
- Internal owner
- Next action

Codex must classify lead candidates as:

- Strong potential lead
- Possible co-lead
- Strategic follower
- Passive follower
- Not currently suitable

### Stage 2 — Commitment Tracking

Goal:

Convert investor interest into explicit commitment status.

Commitment states:

- Not contacted
- Contacted
- Interested
- Meeting scheduled
- First meeting complete
- Follow-up requested
- Data room shared
- Diligence active
- Soft commitment
- Conditional commitment
- Verbal commitment
- Document review
- Signed
- Funded
- Passed
- Deferred
- Unresponsive

Ariyo must not count an investor as committed unless the commitment status is at least `Signed`, unless explicitly reporting a separate soft-circle metric.

### Stage 3 — Diligence Coordination

Goal:

Answer investor diligence quickly without creating contradictory narratives.

Diligence categories:

- Product
- Market
- Customer/pilot proof
- Enterprise readiness
- AI governance
- Security/privacy
- Technical architecture
- Marketplace strategy
- Business model
- Financial model
- Use of funds
- Team
- Legal/corporate
- Competition
- Roadmap
- Risk mitigation

Codex must track:

- Question asked
- Investor source
- Owner
- Evidence source
- Response draft
- Approved response
- Data room artifact link
- Status
- Date answered
- Follow-up needed

### Stage 4 — Terms Alignment

Goal:

Clarify economic, governance, legal, and operational terms before document execution.

Terms may include:

- Round size
- Instrument type
- Valuation or cap logic
- Discount or conversion logic
- Pro rata rights
- Information rights
- Board rights or observer rights
- MFN terms
- Major investor threshold
- Closing conditions
- Minimum close amount
- Side letters
- Strategic rights
- Confidentiality
- Transfer restrictions

Ariyo must escalate any material term change to counsel and founder decision review.

### Stage 5 — Legal Document Workflow

Goal:

Coordinate document drafting, review, signature, and version control.

Required controls:

- Counsel owns legal form
- Founder owns business approval
- Finance owns funds/cap table coordination
- Investor relations owns communication
- No stale document versions circulated
- No unsigned changes treated as final
- No side agreements outside controlled process

Document state model:

- Not started
- Draft requested
- Counsel drafting
- Founder review
- Investor review
- Negotiation active
- Final review
- Ready for signature
- Signed
- Archived

### Stage 6 — Funds Flow & Close Mechanics

Goal:

Confirm documents, wires/funds, acknowledgements, and records.

Required records:

- Investor legal name
- Investor entity
- Contact person
- Email
- Address, if required
- Tax or compliance details, if required by counsel/process
- Commitment amount
- Signed document status
- Funds received status
- Date funds received
- Bank confirmation owner
- Cap table update status
- Receipt acknowledgement status

Codex must not expose banking details in broad documents. Banking details must be handled only in secure, counsel/finance-approved channels.

### Stage 7 — Close Communication

Goal:

Communicate clearly before, during, and after closing without overclaiming.

Communication types:

- Lead investor update
- Soft-circle update
- Closing deadline reminder
- Diligence response
- Signed investor acknowledgement
- Funds received acknowledgement
- Post-close update
- Non-participating investor note
- Internal close status report

Ariyo must avoid public announcement until legal, investor, and founder approval is explicit.

### Stage 8 — Post-Close Operating Handoff

Goal:

Turn capital into accountable execution.

Post-close handoff includes:

- Updated runway model
- Use-of-funds execution plan
- Hiring/contracting priorities
- Product release gates
- Pilot/customer commitments
- Investor reporting cadence
- Governance calendar
- Board/advisor rhythm, if relevant
- Data room archive
- Updated investor CRM
- Post-close weekly update format

## 6. Lead Investor Close Process

### 6.1 Lead Investor Readiness

Before treating an investor as potential lead, Ariyo must know:

- Why this investor fits Ariyo
- Their check size range
- Their decision process
- Whether they can set terms
- Their sector thesis
- Their concerns
- What proof they need
- What timeline they are operating on
- Who else at the firm must approve

### 6.2 Lead Investor Briefing Pack

Ariyo must prepare a controlled lead investor pack:

- One-line thesis
- Category narrative
- Current traction/pilot evidence
- MVP/demo status
- Enterprise readiness status
- Marketplace strategy
- Use of funds
- Round logic
- Key risks and mitigations
- Data room access level
- Meeting agenda

### 6.3 Lead Investor Meeting Flow

Recommended structure:

1. Category framing
2. Why now
3. Product demo or product proof
4. Pilot/customer evidence
5. Business model
6. Enterprise readiness
7. Round purpose
8. What Ariyo is looking for in a lead
9. Investor questions
10. Next step and timeline

### 6.4 Lead Investor Decision Gate

Codex must prompt founders to confirm:

- Is this investor strategically valuable?
- Are the proposed terms acceptable?
- Are there governance concerns?
- Does this investor improve the round?
- Does this investor create future risk?
- What must counsel review?

## 9. Counsel Workflow

### 9.1 Counsel Activation Trigger

Counsel must be activated when:

- Terms are being discussed seriously
- A lead investor emerges
- Side letter requests appear
- Investor asks for legal docs
- Funds flow is being prepared
- Cap table will change
- Public announcement is considered
- Securities/regulatory questions arise

### 9.2 Counsel Workflow Stages

1. Founder prepares business terms summary
2. Counsel reviews structure
3. Counsel drafts or reviews documents
4. Founder reviews business implications
5. Investor counsel review, if any
6. Negotiation changes tracked
7. Final versions locked
8. Signature process launched
9. Funds flow coordinated
10. Closing archive completed

### 9.3 Legal Version Control

Codex must enforce:

- One source of truth for current document version
- No unapproved edits
- No final labels without counsel approval
- No sharing of outdated versions
- No side agreement without record

## 15. Founder Operating Rules During Close

During active close, founders must:

- Keep product execution alive
- Avoid over-customizing narrative per investor
- Avoid saying yes to conflicting expectations
- Keep weekly investor pipeline review
- Keep data room updated
- Keep all commitments tracked
- Separate legal questions from business questions
- Escalate unclear terms
- Preserve customer/pilot momentum
- Avoid public claims before approval

- Send post-close investor update
- Confirm reporting cadence
- Update internal budget/runway view
- Activate use-of-funds plan
- Update product sprint priorities
- Review hiring/vendor decisions
- Confirm pilot/customer commitments
- Archive closing documents

### 16.2 First 30 Days After Close

- Deliver first post-close milestone update
- Show product execution progress
- Show customer/pilot progress
- Show major risk mitigation
- Begin investor value-add asks
- Confirm board/advisor rhythm, if relevant

### 16.3 First 90 Days After Close

- Demonstrate capital-to-progress conversion
- Show roadmap velocity
- Show customer/pilot evidence
- Show revenue or expansion signals, if applicable
- Show product readiness improvements
- Prepare next proof cycle

## 17. Codex Execution Protocol

When Codex uses this skill, it must:

1. Read fundraising readiness skills first.
2. Identify current fundraising stage.
3. Create a close tracker only if terms readiness is sufficient.
4. Separate soft commitments, signed commitments, and funded capital.
5. Never treat investor enthusiasm as a close.
6. Never generate final legal advice.
7. Flag counsel-required issues.
8. Track owner, evidence, and next action for every closing item.
9. Preserve confidentiality.
10. Produce a closing status report after every work unit.

- Cap table updates are required

## 21. Forbidden Patterns

Ariyo must not:

- Count verbal enthusiasm as committed capital
- Share inconsistent terms with different investors
- Accept funds without counsel-approved process
- Send stale legal documents
- Overstate traction or proof
- Hide unresolved risks
- Ignore investor objections
- Let closing tasks live only in founder memory
- Treat fundraising close as separate from product execution
- Use generic startup fundraising templates without Ariyo-specific evidence
- Build investor dashboards with fake metrics
- Mix confidential and public investor materials
- Neglect post-close reporting obligations

---

## 22. Golden Rule

The fundraising close is not the end of fundraising.

It is the beginning of accountability.

Ariyo must close capital in a way that improves trust, preserves founder control, supports product execution, strengthens customer proof, and creates the evidence path for the next stage of company building.
