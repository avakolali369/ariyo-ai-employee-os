# Ariyo Product Requirements MVP Skill

## Purpose

This skill defines the official product requirements for the first sellable MVP of Ariyo.

Ariyo is an AI Employee Operating System, not a chatbot builder, prompt playground, automation toy, or generic AI wrapper. The MVP must prove that a business can hire an AI employee, provide trusted business context, connect at least one usable channel, supervise risky actions, measure useful outcomes, and understand the value of upgrading.

This file converts Ariyo's strategy, architecture, frontend standards, backend standards, AI governance, security, privacy, reliability, analytics, monetization, and customer success contracts into a concrete MVP scope.

## Core Contract

Ariyo MVP is not the smallest version of every idea.

Ariyo MVP is the smallest safe, valuable, measurable, monetizable, and trustable version of the AI Employee Operating System.

The MVP must let a real business reach first value quickly, understand what the AI employee can and cannot do, control cost and risk, supervise sensitive actions, and see measurable outcomes.

The MVP must not ship features that look impressive but cannot be secured, monitored, explained, billed, supported, or safely operated.

## MVP Thesis

The Ariyo MVP succeeds if a small or mid-sized business can:

1. Create an organization.
2. Choose a business goal.
3. Hire a recommended AI employee.
4. Provide business context.
5. Add usable knowledge.
6. Connect or simulate one customer-facing channel.
7. Test the employee safely.
8. Deploy the employee with review controls.
9. Receive and process real or realistic customer requests.
10. Review risky outputs before they are sent or executed.
11. Measure time saved, resolved work, cost, and trust signals.
12. Understand what is included in Basic, Pro, and Smart.
13. Upgrade when more employees, workflows, integrations, intelligence, or automation are needed.

## MVP Product Positioning

### Ariyo MVP is

- A business-ready AI employee platform.
- A guided operating system for hiring and managing AI employees.
- A safe AI execution environment with human review gates.
- A knowledge-aware customer and operations assistant system.
- A measurable productivity and automation product.
- A premium SaaS foundation that can grow into enterprise readiness.

### Ariyo MVP is not

- A generic chatbot builder.
- A prompt marketplace.
- A model comparison playground.
- A no-code automation clone.
- A full CRM.
- A full helpdesk replacement.
- A full enterprise platform on day one.
- An unlimited AI usage product.
- A product that hides AI uncertainty.

## MVP Users

### Primary Buyer

Small and mid-sized business owner, founder, operations lead, agency owner, support manager, or sales manager who wants AI employees to reduce repetitive work and improve response speed.

### Primary Operator

Admin or team lead who configures employees, uploads knowledge, connects channels, reviews outputs, monitors performance, and controls cost.

### Secondary User

Team member who reviews AI drafts, handles escalations, updates knowledge, or monitors assigned workflows.

### End Customer

The business customer's customer who interacts with Ariyo-powered responses through website chat, email, or another connected channel.

## MVP Ideal Customer Profile

The MVP should be optimized for businesses that:

- Have repeated customer questions.
- Have structured knowledge or documents.
- Need faster response times.
- Have limited support or operations staff.
- Can benefit from one or two AI employees.
- Are willing to review AI output before full automation.
- Need visible control over cost and data.
- Want a premium but understandable AI product.

## MVP Plan Scope: Basic, Pro, Smart

Ariyo MVP must make plan differences clear to the customer. Plans are not vague labels. Each plan must define capability, intelligence, automation depth, limits, and cost controls.

### Basic Plan

Basic is for safe entry-level usage.

Basic should provide a useful AI employee experience with strict cost and automation limits.

#### Basic includes

- One organization.
- One workspace.
- One active AI employee.
- Limited AI employee templates.
- Guided onboarding.
- Basic business context setup.
- Manual knowledge upload with limited file count and size.
- Basic RAG retrieval.
- Website chat sandbox or one simple customer-facing channel.
- Human review required for external responses by default.
- Simple conversation/task inbox.
- Basic analytics dashboard.
- Monthly usage budget.
- Usage warnings.
- Hard usage limits.
- Basic model routing.
- Caching for repeated knowledge answers.
- Standard support.

#### Basic restrictions

- No advanced workflows.
- No multi-step autonomous actions.
- No advanced integrations.
- No custom AI employee roles beyond allowed templates.
- No advanced model selection.
- No high-cost reasoning by default.
- No bulk knowledge ingestion.
- No advanced admin roles.
- No advanced audit export.
- No enterprise controls.

#### Basic cost control behavior

- Hard monthly usage cap.
- Conservative model selection.
- Automatic downgrade to lower-cost models when possible.
- Aggressive caching for repeated questions.
- Throttling after abnormal usage.
- Clear upgrade prompts when limits are reached.

### Pro Plan

Pro is for operational business usage.

Pro should provide a serious AI employee system for businesses that want reliable daily operation, multiple employees, better workflows, better analytics, and stronger control.

#### Pro includes

- Multiple AI employees within plan limits.
- Expanded AI employee templates.
- Custom employee configuration.
- More knowledge sources and larger knowledge capacity.
- Improved RAG retrieval.
- Multiple connected channels within plan limits.
- Workflow automation for approved use cases.
- Human review configuration by workflow and risk level.
- Team member roles.
- Admin controls.
- Cost dashboard.
- Per-employee cost attribution.
- Per-workflow cost attribution.
- Usage alerts.
- Budget thresholds.
- Retry and fallback controls.
- Better model routing.
- Customer success playbook visibility.
- Advanced analytics dashboard.
- Priority support compared with Basic.

#### Pro restrictions

- Limited advanced reasoning.
- Limited high-cost model use.
- Limited enterprise admin features.
- Limited audit export.
- Limited custom provider routing.
- Limited advanced compliance controls.
- Limited autonomous execution for high-risk workflows.

#### Pro cost control behavior

- Monthly budget per organization.
- Optional budget per employee.
- Optional budget per workflow.
- Alerts at configurable thresholds.
- Soft throttling before hard cutoff.
- Model downgrade when cost or usage exceeds policy.
- Cost attribution by employee, workflow, channel, and integration.
- Admin-visible usage forecast.

### Smart Plan

Smart is the fullest, most intelligent, and most automated Ariyo experience.

Smart can cost more because it may use stronger models, deeper reasoning, broader retrieval, more automation, more workflows, and more proactive intelligence. Smart must still be governed. Smart is full-option, not unlimited chaos.

#### Smart includes

- Highest plan-level AI employee capacity.
- Advanced AI employee templates.
- Custom AI employee design within safety policies.
- Advanced workflows.
- Advanced RAG and knowledge orchestration.
- Larger knowledge capacity.
- Multi-source retrieval.
- More integrations.
- More channels.
- Advanced model routing.
- Selective high-intelligence model use.
- Deeper reasoning where justified.
- Proactive insights and recommendations.
- Predictive usage forecasting.
- Smart budget recommendations.
- Advanced cost anomaly detection.
- ROI tracking by employee and workflow.
- Advanced human review rules.
- Higher automation limits.
- Advanced audit and operational visibility.
- Priority support.
- Upgrade-ready enterprise controls where applicable.

#### Smart restrictions

- No unlimited ungoverned AI usage.
- No bypassing safety reviews for high-risk actions.
- No unrestricted access to all organization data.
- No hidden model/provider behavior.
- No uncontrolled autonomous spending.
- No tool execution without permission and audit trail.

#### Smart cost control behavior

- Predictive budget controls.
- Anomaly detection for sudden AI cost spikes.
- Automatic workflow pause when cost risk is high.
- Approval gate for unusually expensive operations.
- Intelligent model upgrade only when expected value justifies cost.
- Intelligent model downgrade when cheaper models are sufficient.
- Cost-per-outcome tracking.
- ROI signals for employee and workflow decisions.
- Advanced cost dashboards.
- Admin-defined spending policies.

## MVP Functional Requirements

## 1. Authentication and Organization Setup

### Goal

Allow a new customer to create an organization safely and reach setup quickly.

### Must Have

- Sign up.
- Login.
- Logout.
- Organization creation.
- Workspace creation or default workspace.
- Owner role assignment.
- Basic team invite support or deferred invite placeholder.
- Plan selection or trial assignment.
- Terms and privacy acknowledgement where required.

### Acceptance Criteria

- A user can create an account and organization without technical assistance.
- Every created entity is organization-scoped.
- A user cannot access another organization's data.
- Owner permissions are enforced in the backend.
- Failed auth states are handled with safe copy.

## 2. Guided Onboarding and Activation

### Goal

Move users from sign-up to first useful AI employee outcome quickly.

### Must Have

- Guided setup flow.
- Business goal selection.
- Business context capture.
- Recommended employee suggestion.
- Knowledge setup step.
- Channel setup or sandbox simulation.
- Test mode.
- Deploy readiness checklist.
- First useful output.

### Acceptance Criteria

- A new organization can reach first value in under five minutes when using sample knowledge or sandbox mode.
- Users can skip non-critical setup steps without breaking the product.
- The system clearly shows what remains before deployment.
- Onboarding tracks activation events.
- Empty states explain the next best action.

## 3. AI Employee Hiring

### Goal

Allow customers to hire a clearly defined AI employee rather than configure a generic bot.

### Must Have

- Employee marketplace or template selector.
- Employee role description.
- Capabilities list.
- Limitations list.
- Required knowledge checklist.
- Required channel checklist.
- Default review policy.
- Plan availability indicator.
- Create employee action.

### MVP Employee Templates

At minimum, MVP should include a small set of high-value employee templates:

- Customer Support Assistant.
- Sales Response Assistant.
- Operations FAQ Assistant.

Additional templates may exist only if they are safe, testable, supportable, and clearly scoped.

### Acceptance Criteria

- User understands what the employee does before hiring it.
- User sees plan restrictions before selecting advanced employees.
- Employee cannot act without required configuration.
- Employee actions are tied to permissions, plans, and review policy.

## 4. AI Employee Configuration

### Goal

Let customers configure an employee with business-safe settings.

### Must Have

- Employee name.
- Role.
- Tone.
- Language preference.
- Business context association.
- Knowledge source association.
- Channel association.
- Human review mode.
- Escalation instructions.
- Forbidden topics/instructions.
- Publish/deploy state.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Configuration changes are saved reliably.
- Unsafe or incomplete configuration blocks deployment.
- Users can preview behavior before deployment.
- Changes are auditable.
- Backend enforces allowed states.

## 5. Business Context

### Goal

Give AI employees enough trusted business context to produce relevant outputs.

### Must Have

- Business name.
- Industry.
- Audience/customer type.
- Products/services.
- Tone/brand voice.
- Support or sales goals.
- Escalation rules.
- Operating boundaries.

### Acceptance Criteria

- AI outputs reflect configured business context.
- Missing context is requested in a user-friendly way.
- Context is organization-scoped.
- Sensitive context is not exposed in frontend logs or analytics events.

## 6. Knowledge and RAG

### Goal

Allow the employee to answer from trusted business knowledge.

### Must Have

- File upload or text knowledge input.
- Knowledge source list.
- Processing status.
- Basic chunking and indexing pipeline.
- Retrieval during AI response generation.
- Source-aware answer behavior.
- Knowledge freshness indicator.
- Failed ingestion state.
- Delete knowledge source.

### MVP Knowledge Source Types

- Manual text input.
- PDF/document upload where supported.
- Website URL ingestion may be included only if reliable and safe.
- External knowledge integrations can be later unless already supported safely.

### Acceptance Criteria

- User can add knowledge and see when it is ready.
- Employee can answer from uploaded knowledge.
- Employee admits when knowledge is insufficient.
- Deleted knowledge is no longer used.
- Retrieval events are observable.
- RAG behavior is tested with expected questions.

## 7. Channel and Interaction Inbox

### Goal

Give businesses a safe place to test and process AI-assisted customer interactions.

### Must Have

- Test chat or sandbox channel.
- Conversation list.
- Conversation detail.
- AI draft generation.
- Human approve/edit/reject flow.
- Escalation marker.
- Status: new, drafted, awaiting_review, approved, sent, escalated, closed.

### MVP Channel Scope

The MVP should prioritize one production-ready simple channel and one sandbox/test mode.

Recommended minimum:

- Internal test chat for onboarding and QA.
- Website chat or email-like inbox as the first customer-facing channel.

### Acceptance Criteria

- AI does not send external responses without allowed policy.
- Human can review, edit, and approve drafts.
- Conversation status updates are reliable.
- Draft sources and confidence signals are visible where useful.
- Customer-facing failures are graceful.

## 8. Human Review Governance

### Goal

Prevent unsafe AI actions while allowing useful automation.

### Must Have

- Review queue.
- Review item detail.
- Approve.
- Reject.
- Edit before approval.
- Reason for rejection.
- Review status lifecycle.
- Reviewer identity.
- Audit trail.

### Acceptance Criteria

- High-risk outputs require review.
- Review decision is recorded.
- AI cannot bypass review with prompt instructions.
- User understands why review is required.
- Review events are observable.

## 9. Workflow Automation MVP

### Goal

Allow limited, safe workflow automation without exposing the product to uncontrolled action risk.

### Must Have

- Simple workflow templates.
- Trigger definition.
- AI draft step.
- Human review step.
- Completion step.
- Workflow status.
- Error state.
- Retry where safe.

### MVP Workflow Examples

- Draft answer to support question.
- Draft sales response from product/service info.
- Summarize incoming inquiry.
- Suggest escalation reason.
- Create internal task draft where supported.

### Not MVP

- Fully autonomous multi-system workflows.
- Unrestricted tool chaining.
- Money movement.
- Legal/medical/financial advice automation.
- Destructive actions without review.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Workflow execution is traceable.
- Failed workflow does not create duplicate external actions.
- Workflow cost is attributed.
- Risky workflows route to review.

## 10. Integration MVP

### Goal

Support enough integrations to prove value without creating excessive security, support, and reliability burden.

### Must Have

- Integration provider list.
- Connection state.
- Setup instructions.
- Scope explanation.
- Disconnect action.
- Health state.
- Error state.
- Audit events for connection changes.

### MVP Integration Strategy

MVP integrations should be limited to high-confidence, high-value integrations that can be secured, monitored, and supported.

Recommended MVP priorities:

- Website chat or embedded channel.
- Email-like inbox or inbound message source.
- Manual knowledge upload.
- Optional Google Drive or website ingestion only if stable.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Integrations are organization-scoped.
- Tokens are never exposed to frontend.
- Disconnect behavior is clear.
- Webhooks are verified where used.
- Integration failures have user-safe messages.

## 11. Admin and Permissions

### Goal

Protect organizations, billing, employees, integrations, and review authority.

### Must Have

- Owner role.
- Admin/member/reviewer roles if team support is included.
- Permission checks in backend.
- Permission-aware navigation.
- Permission-denied states.
- Audit logs for sensitive admin actions.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Frontend hides unavailable actions but backend remains source of truth.
- Users cannot self-lockout as only owner.
- Billing/admin/integration/review actions require correct permissions.
- Unauthorized requests fail safely.

## 12. Plan, Billing, and Entitlements

### Goal

Make Basic, Pro, and Smart plan value clear and enforceable.

### Must Have

- Plan record.
- Entitlement checks.
- Usage limits.
- Upgrade prompts.
- Plan comparison.
- Usage dashboard.
- Cost controls.
- Billing state placeholder or live billing if ready.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Plan limits are enforced in backend.
- Users understand why a feature is unavailable.
- Upgrade prompts connect to clear value.
- AI usage cannot exceed allowed budget without policy.
- Smart is full-option but still governed.

## 13. AI Cost Control

### Goal

Prevent AI usage from becoming financially unsafe for Ariyo or surprising for customers.

### Must Have

- Usage tracking.
- Cost attribution by organization.
- Cost attribution by employee.
- Cost attribution by workflow where applicable.
- Plan budgets.
- Alerts.
- Throttling.
- Model downgrade policy.
- Caching for repeated results.
- Abnormal usage detection.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Every AI call has organization, employee, workflow, user, and plan context where available.
- Cost is visible to admins.
- Cost controls are plan-aware.
- Runaway usage is blocked or throttled.
- Smart plan can use more intelligence without uncontrolled spending.

## 14. Analytics and Observability

### Goal

Make activation, product value, AI behavior, cost, errors, and customer health visible.

### Must Have

- Global event envelope.
- Activation events.
- Employee lifecycle events.
- AI execution events.
- RAG events.
- Workflow events.
- Human review events.
- Billing/cost events.
- Security events.
- Reliability events.
- Error taxonomy.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Key user actions emit events.
- Events do not contain raw PII or secrets.
- Events can be correlated across request, execution, workflow, and AI call.
- Dashboards can answer MVP product questions.

## 15. Security and Privacy

### Goal

Ship the MVP with baseline trust protections from day one.

### Must Have

- Organization isolation.
- Backend permission enforcement.
- Secure auth/session handling.
- Token/secret protection.
- No provider secrets in frontend.
- Safe file upload validation.
- Data deletion path.
- Data export path or defined admin process.
- Audit logs for sensitive actions.
- Prompt injection defense basics.
- RAG data exposure checks.
- Logging redaction.

### Acceptance Criteria

- IDOR tests pass for organization-scoped resources.
- Sensitive actions are auditable.
- Uploaded files are validated and size-limited.
- User can request or trigger deletion/export according to product policy.
- AI cannot access data from other organizations.
- Security release gate passes before launch.

## 16. Reliability and SLA Readiness

### Goal

Make MVP reliable enough for real customer use without overpromising enterprise guarantees too early.

### Must Have

- Basic health checks.
- Error monitoring.
- Queue monitoring.
- Retry policy.
- Timeout policy.
- Degraded mode messaging.
- Provider outage handling.
- Backup/restore process.
- Incident severity definitions.
- Rollback process.

### Acceptance Criteria

- AI provider failure does not crash user experience.
- Long-running jobs show status.
- Failed jobs can be retried safely when idempotent.
- Users see clear degraded states.
- Release can be rolled back.

## 17. Quality Assurance

### Goal

Prevent MVP from becoming a fragile demo.

### Must Have

- Unit tests for core logic.
- API contract tests.
- E2E tests for critical journeys.
- Permission tests.
- RAG quality tests.
- AI evaluation set.
- Cost control tests.
- Integration error tests.
- Accessibility checks.
- Performance checks.
- Security smoke tests.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Critical path E2E tests pass.
- AI output quality passes minimum evaluation threshold.
- Release gate checklist is completed.
- No P0/P1 unresolved defects at launch.

## MVP Critical User Journeys

## Journey 1: New Customer Reaches First Value

1. User signs up.
2. User creates organization.
3. User selects business goal.
4. Ariyo recommends AI employee.
5. User hires employee.
6. User enters business context.
7. User uploads knowledge or uses sample knowledge.
8. User tests employee.
9. User reviews AI draft.
10. User deploys employee or continues sandbox testing.
11. User sees first value dashboard.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Journey can be completed without developer help.
- User always knows the next action.
- Missing setup is explained.
- First value is measurable.

## Journey 2: Admin Controls Cost

1. Admin opens usage dashboard.
2. Admin sees plan, budget, and current usage.
3. Admin sees cost by employee.
4. Admin receives warning near threshold.
5. System throttles or downgrades when necessary.
6. Admin sees upgrade option or policy adjustment.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Cost is not hidden.
- Budget events are observable.
- Limits are enforced by backend.
- Upgrade messaging is honest.

## Journey 3: Human Reviews AI Draft

1. Customer message arrives.
2. AI employee generates draft.
3. Draft is routed to review.
4. Reviewer sees context, confidence, and sources where available.
5. Reviewer approves, edits, or rejects.
6. Final response is sent or marked complete.
7. Audit log records decision.

### Acceptance Criteria

- AI cannot bypass required review.
- Reviewer action is traceable.
- Rejected output improves learning/evaluation data where appropriate and privacy-safe.

## Journey 4: Knowledge Gap Is Detected

1. Customer asks a question.
2. RAG retrieval finds insufficient context.
3. AI employee admits uncertainty or escalates.
4. Admin sees knowledge gap.
5. Admin adds missing knowledge.
6. Future answer improves.

### Acceptance Criteria

- AI does not hallucinate as a confident answer.
- Knowledge gaps are measurable.
- Admin has clear next action.

## Journey 5: Plan Upgrade Moment

1. User hits Basic or Pro limit.
2. UI explains what limit was reached.
3. UI explains what the next plan unlocks.
4. User upgrades or stays within limit.
5. Entitlements update safely.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Upgrade prompts are contextual.
- No vague paywall language.
- Backend enforces new entitlements.

- Knowledge-grounded where required.
- Plan-aware.
- Cost-aware.
- Review-aware.
- Safety-evaluated.
- Observable.
- Explicit about uncertainty.
- Unable to bypass permissions or review gates.

AI must not:

- Claim unsupported knowledge.
- Expose internal prompts.
- Access unauthorized organization data.
- Execute tools without permission.
- Ignore budget controls.
- Send high-risk external actions without review.
- Produce legal, medical, financial, or regulated advice as authoritative output.

## MVP Release Gates

The MVP may not launch until all release gates are satisfied.

### Product Gate

- P0 user journeys work end-to-end.
- First value can be reached quickly.
- Plan differences are clear.
- Upgrade moments are understandable.

### Security Gate

- IDOR tests pass.
- Auth/session tests pass.
- Sensitive data is redacted from logs.
- Secrets are not exposed to frontend.
- File upload validation exists.
- Prompt injection baseline tests pass.

### Privacy Gate

- Data ownership is clear.
- Deletion/export process exists.
- Data retention assumptions are documented.
- AI data usage policy is reflected in product behavior.

### AI Quality Gate

- Evaluation set passes minimum threshold.
- RAG answer quality is acceptable.
- Hallucination handling is tested.
- Unsafe output blocking is tested.
- Human review routing is tested.

### Cost Gate

- Every AI call is attributable.
- Plan budgets are enforced.
- Runaway usage is blocked.
- Model fallback/downgrade policy exists.
- Basic/Pro/Smart limits are enforced.

### Reliability Gate

- Health checks exist.
- Queue failure states are handled.
- Provider failure fallback exists.
- Rollback plan exists.
- Critical alerts exist.

### Analytics Gate

- Activation funnel events exist.
- AI execution events exist.
- Cost events exist.
- Review events exist.
- Error events exist.
- Events avoid PII/secrets.

## MVP Risks and Mitigations

## Risk: MVP becomes too broad

### Mitigation

Keep P0 small. Move impressive but non-essential features to P1 or P2.

## Risk: AI output is not trustworthy

### Mitigation

Use knowledge grounding, confidence display, uncertainty handling, evaluation sets, and human review.

## Risk: AI cost becomes uncontrolled

### Mitigation

Use plan limits, budgets, throttling, caching, model downgrade, attribution, and anomaly detection.

## Risk: Integrations slow down launch

### Mitigation

Ship with sandbox/test mode and one reliable production channel before expanding integrations.

## Risk: Security gaps in multi-tenant data

### Mitigation

Prioritize organization isolation, IDOR tests, permission enforcement, audit logs, and security release gates.

## Risk: Users do not understand value

### Mitigation

Guide onboarding to first outcome, show time saved, show resolved work, show next best action, and use customer success playbooks.

## Risk: Smart plan appears unlimited

### Mitigation

Make Smart full-option but governed. Clearly communicate budgets, fair use, high-cost reasoning controls, and approval gates.

## MVP Acceptance Criteria Summary

The MVP is acceptable when:

- A real customer can create an organization.
- A real customer can hire and configure one AI employee.
- The employee can use business context and knowledge.
- The employee can draft useful responses.
- Risky outputs can be reviewed by a human.
- Cost is tracked and controlled.
- Basic, Pro, and Smart are clear.
- Core data is organization-isolated.
- Critical user journeys are tested.
- Events show activation, value, cost, reliability, and errors.
- The system can fail gracefully.
- The team can support, debug, and improve the product.

## Forbidden MVP Patterns

Never ship MVP behavior that includes:

- A generic chatbot as the primary product.
- AI employees without clear role and boundaries.
- External AI responses sent without allowed review policy.
- Tool execution without permission checks.
- Cross-organization data access.
- Raw provider tokens in frontend.
- Hidden AI cost behavior.
- Vague Basic/Pro/Smart differences.
- Unlimited Smart usage without controls.
- Plan limits enforced only in frontend.
- Knowledge deletion that does not affect retrieval.
- Raw PII in analytics events.
- Raw provider errors shown to customers.
- Long-running jobs without visible status.
- Retry logic that creates duplicate external actions.
- Integrations without disconnect and health states.
- Human review without audit trail.
- Security, privacy, cost, or reliability gates skipped for speed.

## Golden Rule

The Ariyo MVP must be small enough to ship, strong enough to trust, useful enough to sell, measurable enough to improve, and safe enough to operate.

## Final Rule

When building MVP features, always choose the path that makes Ariyo more trustworthy, understandable, measurable, and operationally safe.

Do not add a feature to the MVP merely because it is impressive.

Add it only if it helps a real customer hire, trust, use, measure, and pay for an AI employee safely.
