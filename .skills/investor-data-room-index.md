# Ariyo Investor Data Room Index

## Core Contract

Ariyo Investor Data Room Index is not a folder list, file dump, vanity proof archive, or pitch-deck appendix.

It is the governed investor, enterprise-buyer, board, and strategic-partner data room map that tells Ariyo exactly which documents, product assets, pilot evidence, trust artifacts, financial materials, roadmap materials, demo assets, governance proofs, and operational records must exist before serious fundraising, enterprise diligence, strategic partnership review, or acquisition-style evaluation.

Ariyo must not enter investor diligence with scattered documents.
Ariyo must enter diligence with a governed evidence system.

---

## Purpose

This skill defines the canonical structure for Ariyo's investor data room so that every evidence asset is:

- easy to find,
- clearly permissioned,
- mapped to an investor question,
- mapped to a product claim,
- evidence-backed,
- versioned,
- owner-assigned,
- freshness-controlled,
- safe to share,
- and consistent with Ariyo's AI Employee Operating System narrative.

The data room must support:

1. investor due diligence,
2. enterprise buyer review,
3. board-level reporting,
4. strategic partnership evaluation,
5. pilot proof review,
6. sales enablement,
7. legal/compliance review,
8. and internal operating discipline.

---

## Non-Negotiable Rule

Ariyo must never treat the data room as a storage bucket.

The data room is a trust product.

Every file must answer one of these questions:

- Why should Ariyo exist?
- Why now?
- Why this team?
- Why this product architecture?
- Why will customers buy?
- Why will enterprises trust it?
- Why will the marketplace compound?
- Why can Ariyo defend its position?
- Why can Ariyo scale safely?
- Why is this fundable?

## Data Room Operating Principles

### 1. Evidence Before Claims

Ariyo may say only what the data room can support.

If a claim has no evidence, it must be marked as:

- hypothesis,
- roadmap item,
- pilot learning,
- planned capability,
- or not included.

### 2. Freshness Matters

Every data room artifact must include:

- owner,
- date created,
- date last reviewed,
- sharing level,
- source system,
- confidence level,
- and update cadence.

### 3. Permission Matters

Not every investor should see every file.

Ariyo must support access levels:

- Public teaser,
- Intro meeting,
- Active diligence,
- Deep diligence,
- Legal diligence,
- Strategic/enterprise diligence,
- Board/internal only.

### 4. Consistency Matters

The data room, pitch deck, demo, website, product UI, pilot proof, and financial model must not tell different stories.

### 5. No Fake Certainty

Ariyo must distinguish between:

- built,
- demoed,
- piloted,
- used in production,
- revenue-generating,
- contracted,
- planned,
- and aspirational.

## Data Room Permission Levels

### Level 0 — Public

Can be shared openly.

Examples:

- public website narrative,
- public product overview,
- public trust summary,
- public pitch teaser.

### Level 1 — Intro Investor

For first serious investor conversations.

Examples:

- short deck,
- product overview,
- market thesis,
- demo script,
- high-level roadmap.

### Level 2 — Active Diligence

For investors actively evaluating Ariyo.

Examples:

- full deck,
- product demo assets,
- pilot summaries,
- architecture summary,
- financial summary,
- roadmap.

### Level 3 — Deep Diligence

For serious investors close to decision.

Examples:

- detailed pilot evidence,
- detailed financial model,
- technical architecture,
- customer pipeline,
- risk register summary.

### Level 4 — Legal/Financial Diligence

For legal and financial review.

Examples:

- contracts,
- legal policies,
- cap table if applicable,
- incorporation docs,
- accounting summaries,
- compliance documents.

### Level 5 — Internal Only

Not shared unless approved.

Examples:

- raw feedback,
- internal risks,
- unresolved incidents,
- unredacted customer information,
- internal strategy notes.

## Investor Data Room Golden Rule

Ariyo's investor data room must make one thing obvious:

Ariyo is not just building AI features.

Ariyo is building a governed operating system for AI Employees, backed by product architecture, enterprise controls, marketplace compounding, pilot evidence, operational discipline, and a credible path from demo to production.
