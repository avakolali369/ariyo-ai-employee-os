## 1. Purpose

This skill defines Ariyo's official knowledge base, help center, product documentation, support article, troubleshooting, ticket deflection, AI support assistant, escalation, and support-feedback-loop system.

Ariyo is an AI Employee Operating System.

That means support is not only about answering user questions.

Support must help customers:

- understand what AI employees can and cannot do;
- resolve setup, knowledge, integration, workflow, billing, and permission problems;
- recover from stalled onboarding;
- trust automation safely;
- avoid unsafe configuration;
- reduce dependency on human support;
- learn from product guidance;
- connect help content to measurable activation, retention, and expansion.

The knowledge base must be a product system, not a static article repository.

It must power:

```text
help center search
in-product contextual help
AI support assistant grounding
support ticket deflection
customer education paths
customer success playbooks
partner enablement
sales enablement
release notes
incident communication
internal support operations
```

## 4. Strategic Role in Ariyo

The knowledge base and support system must support six business goals:

1. **Activation**  
   Help new users complete setup, hire their first AI employee, connect knowledge, and reach first value.

2. **Trust**  
   Explain safety, review gates, privacy, cost controls, reliability, and AI limitations clearly.

3. **Ticket Deflection**  
   Resolve repetitive issues through articles, contextual help, troubleshooting flows, and AI assistance.

4. **Retention**  
   Detect confusion, failure, stalled usage, and unresolved issues before customers churn.

5. **Expansion**  
   Help successful customers discover advanced employees, workflows, integrations, marketplace assets, and higher plan tiers.

6. **Product Learning**  
   Convert support demand into product improvements, content gaps, education gaps, onboarding improvements, and roadmap signals.

Support content must be treated as a product surface.

## 11. AI Support Assistant

Ariyo may provide an AI support assistant, but it must be grounded, constrained, and escalation-aware.

The AI support assistant must:

- answer from approved knowledge sources;
- cite or link the relevant article where possible;
- respect user role, plan, permissions, and organization context;
- avoid exposing internal-only content;
- avoid revealing sensitive data;
- clarify when it is uncertain;
- escalate when required;
- collect useful diagnostic context;
- never pretend to perform an action it did not perform;
- never override security, billing, privacy, or support escalation rules.

The AI support assistant must not be a generic chatbot.

It must be a product-aware support layer.

---

## 12. AI Support Assistant Answer Policy

The assistant may answer directly when:

- the question is covered by published docs;
- no sensitive account-specific decision is required;
- no security incident is suspected;
- no billing dispute requires human review;
- no legal, compliance, or contract interpretation is requested;
- confidence is high;
- the answer can include next steps or links.

The assistant must escalate or suggest human support when:

- the customer reports possible data loss;
- billing appears incorrect or disputed;
- account access is blocked;
- an integration appears compromised;
- security abuse is suspected;
- an enterprise SLA issue exists;
- an AI employee took or attempted a risky action;
- the answer requires reviewing private customer data;
- the answer depends on internal logs the customer cannot access;
- the user asks for legal, compliance, or contractual advice;
- the assistant confidence is low.

## 13. AI Support Assistant Response Structure

AI support responses should follow this structure:

```text
short answer
likely reason
steps to fix
what to check next
when to escalate
related article or action
```

Example style:

```text
Your AI employee may not be using the latest knowledge because the source has not synced yet or the file is outside its allowed knowledge scope.

Check these three things:
1. Open Knowledge Sources and confirm the source status is Synced.
2. Confirm the AI employee is allowed to use that source.
3. Check whether the answer requires information added after the last sync time.

Escalate to support if the source shows Synced but the AI employee still cannot retrieve known content.
```

Support responses must be calm, specific, and actionable.

They must not overpromise.

## 14. Ticket Deflection System

Ticket deflection must be designed as a helpful resolution system, not a barrier.

Before creating a ticket, Ariyo may show:

- likely matching articles;
- known incidents;
- troubleshooting checklist;
- relevant product status;
- AI support suggestion;
- related Academy lesson;
- diagnostic checklist;
- self-serve action.

However, Ariyo must always provide a clear path to contact support when:

- the customer cannot resolve the issue;
- the issue is high severity;
- trust, billing, security, data, or access is affected;
- the customer is on a plan that includes human support;
- enterprise support commitments require it.

Ticket deflection must be measured by resolved outcomes, not only tickets avoided.

## 16. Support Severity Rules

### SEV 1 — Business Critical

Examples:

- organization cannot access Ariyo;
- major data exposure suspected;
- AI employee executed a high-risk action incorrectly;
- production workflows stopped for many customers;
- billing system incorrectly charges customers at scale.

Required behavior:

- immediate escalation;
- incident review;
- customer communication;
- audit preservation;
- engineering involvement;
- status page consideration.

### SEV 2 — Major Degradation

Examples:

- important integration down for one or more customers;
- workflows delayed significantly;
- knowledge sync repeatedly failing;
- AI provider degraded for Smart customers;
- billing usage dashboard unavailable.

### SEV 3 — Standard Issue

Examples:

- one workflow misconfigured;
- one knowledge source failed;
- user cannot find setting;
- article unclear;
- plan entitlement confusion.

### SEV 4 — Question or Guidance

Examples:

- how-to questions;
- education questions;
- best practices;
- feature availability;
- configuration advice.

## 25. Support Data Privacy

Support systems must follow privacy rules:

- support agents must access only needed account data;
- sensitive logs must be redacted;
- AI support must not expose private organization data;
- support tickets must not contain secrets, API keys, tokens, or raw credentials;
- file attachments must be scanned and controlled;
- internal notes must not be shown to customers;
- support access must be audited;
- customer consent may be required for deeper diagnostics.

## 26. AI Support Data Handling

- customer support messages may be processed only under approved data policy;

- AI must not close high-severity tickets automatically;
- AI must not update account settings without explicit permission and audit logs;
- AI-generated replies must be reviewable for sensitive categories.

AI support tooling must follow `ai-safety-evaluation.md`, `data-governance-privacy.md`, and `human-review-governance.md`.

Support must not only solve tickets.
