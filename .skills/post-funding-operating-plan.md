# Ariyo Post-Funding Operating Plan

## Core Contract

Ariyo Post-Funding Operating Plan is not a generic post-raise checklist, hiring plan, budget spreadsheet, investor update cadence, or founder operating memo.

It is the governed operating system that converts closed funding into measurable product execution, customer progress, team building, financial discipline, governance rhythm, investor accountability, enterprise readiness, marketplace maturity, and production launch momentum.

Ariyo must not treat capital as progress.

Ariyo must turn capital into milestones, evidence, customer value, repeatable systems, and disciplined execution.

---

## 1. Purpose

This skill exists to prevent post-funding drift.

After funding closes, Ariyo must have a clear operating plan for:

- runway protection,
- milestone sequencing,
- hiring discipline,
- product delivery,
- pilot-to-production execution,
- customer commitments,
- investor communication,
- board/investor governance,
- financial controls,
- enterprise trust readiness,
- marketplace scaling,
- operational accountability,
- Codex and AI-assisted implementation order.

The goal is not to spend faster.

The goal is to convert funding into proof that Ariyo can become a category-defining AI Employee Operating System.

---

## 2. Non-Negotiable Post-Funding Rules

### 2.1 Capital Is Not Validation

Funding validates investor belief, not product-market fit.

Ariyo must still prove:

- customers activate,
- AI Employees create measurable value,
- workflows run safely,
- Marketplace assets improve deployment velocity,
- enterprise governance is credible,
- customers renew or expand,
- the platform can scale operationally.

### 2.2 Do Not Hire Ahead of Operating Clarity

Ariyo must not hire roles before:

- the milestone they own is defined,
- the success metric is defined,
- the reporting cadence is defined,
- the manager or owner is defined,
- the first 30/60/90-day mission is defined.

### 2.3 Do Not Build Everything Because Funding Exists

Funding does not unlock unlimited scope.

The MVP Build Scope Lock still governs execution.

The Production Release Gate Checklist still governs launch.

The Production Readiness Gap Audit still governs risk.

### 2.4 Do Not Replace Evidence With Narrative

After funding, every investor update, customer update, and internal review must distinguish:

- completed work,
- shipped work,
- adopted work,
- measured customer value,
- unresolved risk,
- blocked work,
- planned work.

### 2.5 RTL/LTR Law Remains Mandatory

Persian/Farsi and Arabic interfaces must be RTL and right-aligned across all UI elements.

English and European LTR interfaces must be LTR and left-aligned across all UI elements.

This applies to product UI, investor dashboards, customer portals, internal tools, reports, onboarding flows, support messages, demo surfaces, and all post-funding execution artifacts.

## 4. Post-Funding Operating Phases

### Phase 0 — Close Confirmation

Goal: Confirm the funding round is actually closed and operationally usable.

Required outputs:

- final signed documents,
- funds received or legally committed,
- cap table updated,
- investor list confirmed,
- reporting obligations documented,
- board/advisor obligations defined,
- restricted commitments documented,
- use-of-funds envelope approved,
- internal operating cadence activated.

Do not announce or spend as though the round is closed until this phase is complete.

### Phase 1 — Operating Reset

Goal: Reset the company operating system after the raise.

Required outputs:

- post-funding strategic memo,
- 12-month operating priorities,
- 6-month milestone plan,
- 90-day execution plan,
- hiring sequence,
- customer/pilot commitments,
- production readiness targets,
- investor update cadence,
- financial control cadence.

### Phase 2 — MVP to Pilot Expansion

Goal: Move from MVP/demo readiness toward repeatable customer pilots.

Required outputs:

- pilot customer pipeline,
- onboarding capacity model,
- support escalation process,
- customer success ownership,
- feedback-to-roadmap loop,
- pilot success reports,
- conversion criteria.

### Phase 3 — Production Hardening

Goal: Convert demo/MVP surfaces into production-safe systems.

Required outputs:

- backend/API contract completion,
- security remediation plan,
- observability setup,
- audit logs,
- data governance controls,
- AI Employee runtime safeguards,
- Marketplace install safeguards,
- billing/entitlement reliability,
- release gates passed.

### Phase 4 — Enterprise Readiness

Goal: Prepare Ariyo for enterprise procurement, security review, and deployment.

Required outputs:

- Trust Center readiness,
- enterprise admin controls,
- SSO/SCIM roadmap or implementation,
- audit evidence readiness,
- data residency posture,
- support SLA posture,
- enterprise onboarding methodology,
- executive business review motion.

### Phase 5 — Marketplace Growth

Goal: Turn the Marketplace from demo asset into scalable ecosystem.

Required outputs:

- template quality standards,
- creator onboarding,
- template review workflow,
- certification labels,
- install entitlement checks,
- marketplace search quality,
- template health monitoring,
- partner enablement.

### Phase 6 — Fundraising Proof Loop

Goal: Continue converting operating progress into investor confidence.

Required outputs:

- monthly investor updates,
- quarterly proof pack refresh,
- customer proof updates,
- pipeline health updates,
- product milestone evidence,
- use-of-funds progress report,
- risks and mitigation log.

## 6. 30/60/90-Day Post-Funding Plan

### First 30 Days — Reset and Lock

Primary goal: Convert funding into disciplined execution boundaries.

Must complete:

- post-funding operating memo,
- budget envelope,
- hiring sequence,
- MVP scope reconfirmation,
- production gap audit review,
- release gate owner assignment,
- pilot customer target list,
- investor update schedule,
- weekly execution review rhythm,
- Codex workstream activation.

Do not expand roadmap in the first 30 days unless a release gate requires it.

### Days 31–60 — Build and Prove

Primary goal: Ship the MVP surfaces and pilot readiness systems.

Must complete:

- frontend MVP surfaces,
- mock API layer stabilization,
- backend contract review,
- first pilot onboarding motion,
- Customer Success Console readiness,
- internal ops command center readiness,
- Trust Center preview,
- investor progress update with evidence.

### Days 61–90 — Pilot and Harden

Primary goal: Run real pilots and harden production path.

Must complete:

- pilot launch with selected customers,
- feedback-to-roadmap process,
- production gap closure plan,
- launch readiness command center usage,
- release gate status review,
- first post-pilot proof artifacts,
- investor proof update,
- hiring plan adjustment.

## 7. Hiring Sequence

Ariyo must hire in dependency order, not ego order.

### 7.1 First Hires or Contractors

Priority roles:

1. Frontend/product engineer capable of implementing the design system and MVP surfaces.
2. Backend/API engineer capable of productionizing mock contracts.
3. Product/design systems owner capable of maintaining Figma-to-frontend consistency.
4. Customer success/operator capable of running pilots and capturing evidence.
5. Security/infra advisor or fractional expert for production readiness.

### 7.2 Roles to Delay Until Evidence Exists

Delay hiring for:

- large sales team,
- full marketing team,
- large marketplace operations team,
- large creator success team,
- executive roles without operating load,
- regional expansion roles,
- enterprise procurement roles before enterprise demand exists.

### 7.3 Hiring Gate Object

Every hire must include:

```yaml
role:
owner:
why_now:
linked_milestone:
first_30_day_outcome:
first_60_day_outcome:
first_90_day_outcome:
budget_source:
manager_or_review_owner:
kill_or_pause_condition:
```
