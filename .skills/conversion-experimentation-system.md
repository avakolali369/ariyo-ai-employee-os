# Ariyo Conversion Experimentation System Skill

## Status

Production skill.

This skill defines how Ariyo must design, run, measure, govern, and learn from conversion experiments across the public website, pricing page, signup flow, demo funnel, onboarding, product-led growth flows, GEO pages, marketplace, customer lifecycle, and revenue journeys.

This skill must be used whenever Ariyo changes a page, CTA, offer, pricing message, onboarding path, signup friction, demo motion, trial flow, upgrade prompt, lead capture, or conversion-critical product experience.

## 1. Purpose

Ariyo is an AI Employee Operating System.

Ariyo cannot grow professionally by guessing which messages, pages, CTAs, offers, or flows convert customers.

This skill defines the experimentation system that turns conversion work into measurable learning.

The goal is not to run random A/B tests.

The goal is to improve customer understanding, trust, activation, revenue, retention, and upgrade readiness without harming brand quality, accessibility, privacy, pricing integrity, or customer trust.

Ariyo experimentation must improve:

- visitor-to-lead conversion
- visitor-to-trial conversion
- visitor-to-demo conversion
- trial-to-activation conversion
- activation-to-paid conversion
- Basic-to-Pro upgrade conversion
- Pro-to-Smart upgrade conversion
- demo-to-pilot conversion
- pilot-to-paid conversion
- marketplace install conversion
- GEO API lead conversion
- Trust Center assisted conversion
- pricing comprehension
- product category clarity
- onboarding completion
- first value moment speed
- revenue quality
- customer fit

## 2. Core Contract

Ariyo conversion experimentation is not changing button colors until metrics move.

Ariyo conversion experimentation is the disciplined operating system that tests customer understanding, trust, value clarity, pricing confidence, product readiness, and revenue behavior through safe, measurable, reversible experiments.

Every Ariyo experiment must have a hypothesis, target audience, conversion goal, guardrail metrics, owner, exposure rule, measurement plan, rollback path, learning summary, and decision rule.

Ariyo must optimize for durable customer value, not short-term clicks.

## 4. Experimentation Principles

### 4.1 Customer Understanding Before Conversion Tricks

Ariyo must prioritize experiments that answer:

- Does the visitor understand what Ariyo is?
- Does the visitor understand why AI employees are different from chatbots?
- Does the visitor understand what Basic, Pro, and Smart mean?
- Does the visitor trust Ariyo with business data?
- Does the visitor see a use case that matches their business?
- Does the visitor know what to do next?
- Does the visitor understand the value before seeing the price?
- Does the buyer believe Ariyo can create measurable business outcomes?

### 4.2 Revenue Quality Over Raw Conversion

A variant that increases signups but brings low-fit users, support burden, refund risk, or churn risk is not a success.

Ariyo must measure downstream quality:

- activation rate
- first value completion
- demo qualification rate
- trial-to-paid conversion
- plan quality
- usage quality
- AI cost-to-revenue ratio
- support burden
- refund rate
- churn risk
- sales cycle quality
- customer fit

### 4.3 Trust Guardrails Are Mandatory

Ariyo must never test:

- exaggerated AI claims
- fake social proof
- hidden pricing
- misleading discounts
- false urgency
- fake security/compliance claims
- fake uptime claims
- unverified customer logos
- dark patterns
- consent manipulation
- confusing cancellation flows
- unclear plan limitations
- bait-and-switch feature access

### 4.4 Experiments Must Be Reversible

Every experiment must have:

- feature flag
- traffic allocation rule
- rollback trigger
- monitoring dashboard
- owner
- start time
- expected end condition
- risk classification

### 4.5 Experimentation Must Not Break UX Quality

All variants must pass:

- responsive QA
- accessibility QA
- performance budget
- content clarity review
- legal/pricing review when applicable
- analytics instrumentation review
- security/privacy review when applicable

## 6. Experiment Lifecycle

Every experiment must follow this lifecycle.

### 6.1 Intake

Experiment proposal must include:

```json
{
  "name": "homepage_hero_ai_employee_positioning",
  "owner": "growth",
  "surface": "homepage",
  "hypothesis": "If we explain Ariyo as hiring AI employees instead of using AI tools, qualified visitors will better understand the product and click the primary CTA more often.",
  "target_audience": "new visitors from paid search and organic AI automation queries",
  "primary_metric": "primary_cta_click_rate",
  "secondary_metrics": ["pricing_page_visit_rate", "demo_booking_start_rate"],
  "guardrail_metrics": ["bounce_rate", "page_load_time", "signup_activation_rate"],
  "risk_level": "medium",
  "planned_duration": "until sample threshold or maximum 21 days",
  "rollback_trigger": "primary_cta_click_rate drops > 20% and bounce_rate rises > 15% for 48 hours"
}
```

### 6.2 Prioritization

Prioritize experiments using:

- expected customer impact
- revenue impact
- confidence
- ease of implementation
- risk level
- learning value
- dependency readiness
- affected surfaces
- plan-tier relevance

Ariyo may use an ICE-style score internally, but final priority must consider risk and trust.

### 6.3 Design

Experiment design must define:

- control variant
- treatment variants
- audience eligibility
- traffic allocation
- exclusion rules
- randomization unit
- metric definitions
- event instrumentation
- sample requirement
- duration cap
- decision rule

### 6.4 Build

Experiments must be implemented with:

- feature flags
- typed experiment IDs
- deterministic assignment
- analytics events
- accessibility-safe variants
- performance-safe variants
- rollback support
- QA checklist

### 6.5 QA

Before launch, verify:

- all variants render correctly
- mobile/responsive behavior works
- page speed budget is not broken
- events fire correctly
- attribution parameters are preserved
- consent rules are respected
- pricing/legal copy is approved
- fallback behavior works
- no raw PII is captured in events

### 6.6 Launch

Experiment launch must include:

- start timestamp
- owner
- dashboard link
- exposure percentage
- risk level
- expected review cadence
- rollback owner

### 6.7 Monitor

Monitor:

- exposure balance
- event quality
- conversion metrics
- guardrail metrics
- performance impact
- error rates
- accessibility issues
- customer complaints
- sales/support feedback

### 6.8 Decide

Decision outcomes:

```text
ship_winner
keep_control
iterate
extend_test
segment_result
stop_for_risk
inconclusive
```

### 6.9 Document Learning

Every completed experiment must have a learning summary:

```json
{
  "experiment_id": "exp_homepage_hero_001",
  "decision": "ship_winner",
  "winner": "variant_b",
  "primary_metric_result": "+14.2%",
  "guardrail_status": "passed",
  "key_learning": "Qualified visitors responded better to outcome-based AI employee framing than generic AI automation copy.",
  "follow_up_experiments": ["pricing_page_plan_clarity_002"],
  "shipped_at": "2026-07-15T10:00:00Z"
}
```

## 7. Experiment Governance

### 7.1 Risk Levels

#### Low Risk

Examples:

- button label variations
- section ordering
- headline variants with same claim
- FAQ order
- visual hierarchy changes

Approval: growth/product owner.

#### Medium Risk

Examples:

- pricing page layout
- plan comparison messaging
- demo qualification flow
- signup flow friction
- upgrade prompt behavior
- Trust Center placement

Approval: product + growth + relevant domain owner.

#### High Risk

Examples:

- pricing amount changes
- entitlement changes
- billing terms
- security/compliance claims
- legal copy
- AI capability claims
- cancellation/downgrade flows
- consent flows
- data usage explanations

Approval: product + legal/compliance review + security/privacy review + business owner.

### 7.2 Mandatory Guardrails

All experiments must track at least one guardrail metric from each relevant group.

#### Customer Trust Guardrails

- complaint rate
- support ticket rate
- refund request rate
- demo no-show rate
- post-signup confusion rate
- sales objection frequency

#### Product Quality Guardrails

- activation rate
- first value completion rate
- onboarding completion rate
- feature usage quality
- retained usage after 7/14/30 days

#### Revenue Guardrails

- plan quality
- trial-to-paid rate
- upgrade rate
- discount dependency
- revenue per activated account
- AI cost-to-revenue ratio

#### Technical Guardrails

- page load time
- Core Web Vitals equivalent metrics
- API error rate
- form submission errors
- analytics event loss
- frontend exception rate

#### Privacy/Security Guardrails

- PII leakage in events
- consent violations
- sensitive data exposure
- unauthorized feature access
- suspicious bot traffic

## 12. Frontend Implementation Rules

### 12.1 Experiment Assignment

Frontend must not randomly assign variants on every render.

Assignment must be:

- deterministic
- stable per user/session
- server-authoritative when possible
- logged once per exposure
- resilient to reloads
- consent-aware

### 12.2 Rendering

All experiment variants must:

- use existing design tokens
- use approved components
- remain responsive
- preserve accessibility
- avoid layout shift
- avoid hydration mismatch
- preserve SEO/GEO content validity
- avoid breaking schema markup

### 12.3 Feature Flags

Each experiment must have:

- kill switch
- allocation control
- segment rules
- start/end controls
- audit log

### 12.4 Copy Experiments

Copy variants must be stored as structured configs, not scattered hardcoded strings.

Bad:

```tsx
<h1>{Math.random() > 0.5 ? 'AI for your business' : 'Hire AI employees'}</h1>
```

Good:

```tsx
<HeroTitle>{experiment.config.hero_headline}</HeroTitle>
```

## 13. Backend Implementation Rules

Backend must provide:

- experiment registry
- assignment service
- exposure logging
- conversion attribution
- audience segmentation
- result aggregation
- guardrail monitoring
- audit logs
- rollback controls
- permission checks

Backend must not expose experiment admin actions to unauthorized users.

## 14. Attribution Rules

Ariyo must support:

- first-touch attribution
- last-touch attribution
- experiment exposure attribution
- assisted conversion attribution
- sales-assisted conversion attribution
- product-led attribution
- lifecycle attribution

Experiment reporting must avoid overclaiming.

If a visitor saw multiple experiments, attribution must be labeled as influenced, not certain.

## 15. Segmentation Rules

Experiments may be segmented by:

- traffic source
- device type
- country/locale
- visitor type
- ICP segment
- business size
- industry
- plan
- activation stage
- customer lifecycle stage
- partner channel
- marketplace source
- GEO intent

But Ariyo must not segment using sensitive attributes unless there is a lawful, ethical, and documented reason.

Basic-focused experiments should optimize:

- clarity
- low-risk first step
- simple onboarding
- first AI employee value
- safe budget control
- upgrade education

Basic must feel like a real starting point.

### 16.2 Pro Experiments

Pro-focused experiments should optimize:

- operational value
- team workflows
- integrations
- analytics
- reliable daily usage
- cost control
- business outcomes

Pro must feel like the natural plan for serious teams.

### 16.3 Smart Experiments

Smart-focused experiments should optimize:

- advanced intelligence
- automation depth
- GEO capabilities
- predictive insights
- advanced model usage
- marketplace power
- enterprise-grade control

Smart must feel premium and full-option, but never unlimited or uncontrolled.

```text
enterprise_demo_booking_rate
```

Guardrails:

```text
pricing_page_completion_rate
page_load_time
trust_claim_compliance_review_status
```

## 18. Statistical Discipline

Ariyo must not declare winners prematurely.

Experiment analysis must consider:

- sample size
- exposure balance
- confidence level or credible interval
- practical significance
- segment consistency
- novelty effect
- seasonality
- traffic mix changes
- bot traffic
- instrumentation quality
- guardrail impact

A small lift that damages activation quality is not a win.

A statistically unclear result with strong qualitative learning may still justify iteration.

## 19. Heatmap and Session Insight Rules

Heatmaps and session recordings may be used only if:

- consent requirements are satisfied
- sensitive fields are masked
- passwords/payment fields are never recorded
- customer content is not exposed
- internal access is permissioned
- retention is limited
- access is audited

Use heatmaps to understand:

- scroll drop-off
- CTA discoverability
- pricing confusion
- form friction
- mobile usability
- trust section engagement
- GEO API interaction friction

Do not use heatmaps as the only basis for shipping major changes.

- conversion drops materially
- guardrail metric fails
- experiment event loss occurs
- variant error rate rises
- page performance worsens
- checkout errors increase
- signup errors increase
- demo booking errors increase
- bot traffic spikes

## 23. AI-Assisted Experimentation

Ariyo may use AI to help:

- generate hypotheses
- summarize experiment results
- detect anomaly patterns
- identify funnel drop-offs
- suggest copy variants
- map objections to page changes
- generate landing page variants
- cluster qualitative feedback

AI must not autonomously launch experiments.

AI-generated experiment suggestions must be reviewed by a human owner.

AI must not generate misleading claims or unapproved pricing/legal copy.

## 24. Privacy and Consent

Experimentation must obey:

- consent state
- data minimization
- PII redaction
- retention limits
- organization boundaries
- user deletion/export rights
- support access governance

Anonymous website analytics must be separated from authenticated customer analytics unless properly consented and legally permitted.

## 25. SEO and GEO Safety

Experiments must not break:

- canonical URLs
- metadata
- structured data
- sitemap logic
- robots rules
- page content stability for key pages
- GEO answer-ready sections
- Trust Center indexability rules
- legal page integrity

For SEO/GEO-sensitive pages, Ariyo must avoid constantly rotating content in ways that confuse crawlers or AI retrieval systems.

## 26. Performance Budget

No experiment may materially degrade:

- initial page load
- interaction latency
- layout stability
- mobile performance
- form responsiveness
- demo booking reliability
- checkout reliability

Experiment SDKs must be lightweight and non-blocking.

Avoid client-side flicker.

Prefer server-side or edge assignment for high-visibility pages.

## 27. Security Rules

Experiment configuration must not allow:

- arbitrary script injection
- unsafe HTML injection
- unvalidated redirects
- exposure of internal feature flags
- entitlement bypass
- pricing bypass
- unauthorized checkout variants
- admin-only content leakage

Experiment configuration is production behavior and must be treated as sensitive.

## 28. QA Checklist

Before launch:

- hypothesis documented
- metrics defined
- analytics events verified
- variants reviewed
- mobile tested
- accessibility tested
- performance tested
- legal/pricing reviewed if needed
- security/privacy reviewed if needed
- rollback path confirmed
- owner assigned
- dashboard ready
- alerts configured

After completion:

- result summarized
- decision recorded
- winner shipped or reverted
- analytics cleaned up
- learnings added to knowledge base
- follow-up experiments proposed

## 29. Forbidden Patterns

Never:

- run experiments without a hypothesis
- optimize only for clicks
- test fake urgency
- hide pricing truth
- hide cancellation terms
- make unverified AI claims
- make unverified security claims
- use fake testimonials
- use misleading plan comparisons
- run pricing experiments without governance
- expose users to broken variants
- assign variants randomly on every render
- ignore guardrail metrics
- declare winners from tiny samples
- keep losing variants live
- collect PII unnecessarily
- record sensitive session data
- use heatmaps without consent controls
- let AI launch experiments automatically
- let experiments bypass entitlements
- let variants break accessibility
- let variants break performance budgets
- ship experiment code without cleanup

## 30. Golden Rule

Ariyo experimentation must make the product easier to understand, easier to trust, easier to try, easier to buy, and easier to succeed with.

If an experiment increases conversion by reducing honesty, clarity, accessibility, privacy, or trust, it is a failed experiment.

## 31. Final Rule

Every conversion experiment must answer one business question and one customer question.

Business question:

```text
Does this improve qualified growth, revenue, activation, retention, or expansion?
```

Customer question:

```text
Does this help the right customer make a better decision with more confidence?
```

If the answer to either question is unclear, Ariyo must not ship the experiment as a permanent change.
