# Ariyo Trust Center Mockup Specification

## Core Contract

Ariyo Trust Center Mockup Specification is not a generic security page.

It is the governed mockup specification for Ariyo's public and enterprise-facing trust surface: the place where prospects, customers, enterprise buyers, security reviewers, compliance teams, legal teams, procurement stakeholders, investors, and internal operators understand Ariyo's security posture, compliance evidence, data governance model, incident transparency, uptime status, marketplace trust controls, AI Employee governance, and enterprise readiness.

The Trust Center must make Ariyo feel trustworthy before a sales call, credible during procurement, auditable during enterprise review, and operationally transparent after launch.

Ariyo must not hide trust behind marketing claims. Ariyo must expose trust as a product surface.

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this mockup specification is to define the first high-confidence Trust Center and Public Status mockup for Ariyo.

This surface must translate Ariyo's trust, safety, governance, compliance, security, reliability, privacy, data residency, marketplace review, enterprise readiness, and operational status systems into a clear product experience.

The mockup must support:

- Enterprise buyer confidence.
- Investor-grade trust narrative.
- Security and compliance review readiness.
- Procurement acceleration.
- Customer transparency.
- Marketplace trust visibility.
- AI Employee governance credibility.
- Regional and multilingual readiness.
- Public status and incident communication.
- Trust Center evolution into a real operational product area.

## 5. Global RTL/LTR Layout Law

This mockup must follow Ariyo's global bidirectional layout law.

### 5.1 Persian and Arabic

For Persian/Farsi and Arabic interfaces:

- The entire layout must be RTL.
- Text must be right-aligned.
- Navigation must mirror directionally.
- Sidebar or section navigation must appear on the right when appropriate.
- Tables must align labels, headers, and primary text to the right.
- Status cards must preserve logical reading order from right to left.
- Icon placement must be mirrored.
- Timeline direction must be right-to-left unless the timeline represents global chronological standards where explicit dates clarify ordering.
- Breadcrumbs must be RTL.
- Form labels, inputs, evidence request fields, and legal notices must be right-aligned.

### 5.2 English and European LTR Languages

For English and European LTR languages:

- The entire layout must be LTR.
- Text must be left-aligned.
- Navigation must be left-to-right.
- Tables must align labels, headers, and primary text to the left.
- Cards, timelines, and evidence flows must preserve left-to-right scan patterns.

### 5.3 Non-Negotiable Rule

If localization creates a conflict between a generic component pattern and RTL/LTR correctness, RTL/LTR correctness wins.

## 20. Content and Copywriting Rules

Trust Center copy must be precise.

### 20.1 Voice

Use:

- Clear.
- Evidence-based.
- Measured.
- Enterprise-ready.
- Transparent.
- Actionable.

Avoid:

- Hype.
- Guarantees without evidence.
- Legal conclusions.
- Fake certifications.
- Empty claims.
- “Military-grade” or “bank-level” clichés.

### 20.2 Example Copy

Good:

- `Ariyo provides role-based controls for AI Employee creation, deployment, and monitoring.`
- `Some evidence documents may require enterprise access or security review approval.`
- `Marketplace templates disclose required permissions before install.`
- `Workspace-level audit exports are available in enterprise contexts.`

Bad:

- `Fully compliant with every regulation.`
- `100% secure AI automation.`
- `Enterprise-grade by default` without details.
- `SOC 2 certified` unless actually true.

## 26. Mockup Acceptance Criteria

The mockup is acceptable when:

- A buyer can understand Ariyo's trust posture within 30 seconds.
- A security reviewer can find evidence request paths quickly.
- A compliance reviewer can distinguish public, gated, planned, and unavailable evidence.
- A customer admin can understand current system status.
- Marketplace trust is visible and defined.
- AI Employee governance is clearly differentiated from generic AI tools.
- Legal and policy documents are easy to find.
- RTL/LTR behavior is fully specified.

## 29. Golden Rule

Ariyo Trust Center must make trust operational.

Every claim should map to a product control, evidence state, owner, timestamp, policy, governance mechanism, or request path.

If a claim cannot be supported, scoped, requested, or governed, it should not appear in the mockup.
