# Ariyo MVP Demo Flow Script

## Core Contract

Ariyo MVP Demo Flow Script is not a generic product tour, sales script, investor pitch, or screen-by-screen walkthrough.

It is the governed demo narrative that tells Ariyo founders, sales teams, customer success teams, designers, frontend engineers, Codex, and AI-assisted builders exactly how to demonstrate the Ariyo MVP as a credible AI Employee Operating System with Marketplace, governance, trust, outcomes, and enterprise readiness.

The demo must show Ariyo as a serious operating platform for accountable AI employees, not as a chatbot, prompt library, automation toy, or generic SaaS dashboard.

Every demo moment must answer one question: why should a serious team trust Ariyo to run AI work?

## 2. Demo Positioning

Ariyo should be introduced as:

> Ariyo is an AI Employee Operating System that lets teams create, govern, deploy, monitor, and improve AI employees across real business workflows, with an enterprise-ready marketplace, trust layer, and measurable outcome tracking.

Do not introduce Ariyo as:

- a chatbot platform;
- an AI assistant app;
- an automation builder;
- a prompt marketplace;
- an agent playground;
- a low-code workflow tool;
- a marketplace of templates only;
- a dashboard product only;
- a consulting wrapper around AI.

The first sentence of the demo must make the category clear.

## 3. Demo Audience Modes

The same MVP can be demoed in different modes.

### 3.1 Investor Demo Mode

Goal: prove category, product depth, execution seriousness, marketplace potential, enterprise readiness, and monetization path.

Emphasize:

- category creation;
- AI Employee OS;
- marketplace flywheel;
- governance moat;
- enterprise expansion;
- outcome intelligence;
- pricing packaging;
- future scalability.

Avoid:

- too much implementation detail;
- too many settings;
- technical API explanations;
- unresolved compliance claims;
- pretending certifications exist if not yet obtained.

### 3.2 Customer Discovery Demo Mode

Goal: validate pain, workflow fit, buying interest, and MVP usefulness.

Emphasize:

- problems with unmanaged AI work;
- how an AI Employee is created;
- how workflows become accountable;
- how managers approve risky actions;
- how outcomes are tracked;
- how templates accelerate setup.

Avoid:

- investor-style TAM narrative;
- excessive marketplace economics;
- abstract platform language;
- unsupported enterprise claims.

### 3.3 Enterprise Buyer Demo Mode

Goal: prove governance, security posture, admin controls, auditability, and safe rollout.

Emphasize:

- Enterprise Admin;
- roles and permissions;
- SSO/SCIM preview;
- approval gates;
- audit logs;
- Trust Center;
- launch readiness;
- private catalog controls;
- data residency language.

Avoid:

- casual creator economy framing;
- unclear permissions;
- one-click install without review;
- hidden AI cost behavior.

### 3.4 Internal Team Demo Mode

Goal: align product, design, engineering, sales, and operations.

Emphasize:

- MVP scope;
- page order;
- shared components;
- mock data model;
- state coverage;
- what is real versus mocked;
- implementation gates.

## 4. Demo Story Arc

The Ariyo MVP demo must follow this arc:

1. The market problem: AI work is spreading, but companies cannot govern it.
2. The category: Ariyo turns AI from assistants into accountable employees.
3. The operating system: teams can create, manage, monitor, and improve AI employees.
4. The builder: a business user can define role, tools, knowledge, permissions, and outcomes.
5. The workflow layer: AI employees operate inside approved business workflows.
6. The marketplace: teams install trusted templates instead of starting from zero.
7. The governance layer: admins control risk, access, policies, approvals, and audit.
8. The trust layer: buyers see evidence, status, security posture, and readiness.
9. The launch layer: internal teams know whether the product is ready to pilot, demo, or scale.
10. The business model: Basic, Pro, Smart, and Enterprise create upgrade paths.

Do not demo pages as independent screens.

Each page must answer the next obvious question raised by the previous page.

## 6. Global Demo Rules

### 6.1 Product Truth Rules

The demo may show:

- realistic mock data;
- prototype-level interactions;
- demo-mode workflows;
- simulated AI runs;
- representative trust controls;
- planned enterprise controls clearly labeled as preview;
- marketplace templates with demo metadata.

The demo must not claim:

- verified compliance certifications unless actually obtained;
- production uptime unless actually measured;
- live integrations unless actually implemented;
- autonomous high-risk decisions without approval;
- guaranteed ROI;
- unlimited AI cost control;
- full marketplace liquidity before launch;
- fully automated legal/compliance approval.

### 6.2 Visual Rules

The demo must feel:

- premium;
- enterprise-ready;
- calm;
- structured;
- trustworthy;
- data-rich but not noisy;
- operational;
- modern;
- explainable.

The demo must not feel:

- playful like a consumer chatbot;
- cluttered like a legacy admin panel;
- vague like a pitch deck only;
- cheap like a template gallery;
- over-animated;
- visually dependent on fake AI magic.

### 6.3 RTL/LTR Law

Persian/Farsi and Arabic demo surfaces must be fully RTL and right-aligned across all UI elements.

English and European LTR demo surfaces must be fully LTR and left-aligned across all UI elements.

This applies to:

- navigation;
- cards;
- tables;
- forms;
- charts;
- sidebars;
- modals;
- toasts;
- breadcrumbs;
- button groups;
- stepper flows;
- timeline events;
- audit logs;
- empty states;
- error states;
- responsive mobile layouts.

Codex must not fake RTL by only changing text direction. Layout, spacing, alignment, icon placement, progress direction, drawer direction, and table alignment must also adapt.

## 12. Objection Handling

### Objection: Is this just another chatbot?

Response:

> No. A chatbot answers messages. Ariyo creates governed AI employees with job roles, tools, memory, permissions, workflows, approvals, outcomes, cost controls, and audit trails.

Show:

- AI Employee Builder;
- Workflow Builder;
- AI Employee Detail;
- Enterprise Admin.

### Objection: How is this different from automation tools?

Response:

> Automation tools move tasks from step to step. Ariyo manages AI employees that reason, use knowledge, operate tools, request approval, and are measured against business outcomes.

Show:

- workflow step with AI Employee assignment;
- simulation;
- approval gate;
- outcome tracking.

### Objection: Can enterprises trust it?

Response:

> Ariyo is designed around governance first: roles, permissions, policy engine, approvals, audit logs, trust center, marketplace controls, and launch readiness gates.

Show:

- Enterprise Admin;
- Trust Center;
- Template Detail permissions;
- Launch Readiness.

### Objection: Is the marketplace safe?

Response:

> Ariyo Marketplace is not a prompt gallery. Templates include trust labels, permissions, dependencies, reviews, versions, install eligibility, approval states, and region compatibility.

Show:

- Marketplace Homepage;
- Template Detail;
- Creator Portal preview if needed.

### Objection: How do you control AI cost?

Response:

> Ariyo plans include usage boundaries and cost visibility. AI Employees and workflows show cost impact, and admins can control usage by plan, workspace, role, and policy.

Show:

- Pricing;
- Dashboard cost trend;
- AI Employee Detail cost tab;
- Enterprise Admin billing/entitlements.

### Objection: What is actually MVP versus future vision?

Response:

> The MVP focuses on core surfaces: landing, pricing, dashboard, AI Employee creation and detail, workflow builder, marketplace discovery, template detail, enterprise admin preview, trust center preview, and launch readiness. Advanced marketplace operations, partner operations, and deep enterprise automation can be shown as preview states.

Show:

- Launch Readiness;
- MVP Build Scope Lock if internal.
