# Ariyo Landing Page Mockup Specification

## Core Contract

Ariyo Landing Page Mockup Specification is not a generic SaaS homepage brief.

It is the governed mockup specification for the first public-facing product surface that explains Ariyo as an AI Employee Operating System with an Enterprise-ready Marketplace, trust layer, governance model, and measurable business-outcome promise.

The landing page must make Ariyo understandable in the first screen, credible by the second screen, differentiated by the middle of the page, and conversion-ready by the end.

Ariyo must not look like another chatbot, automation tool, prompt library, or AI wrapper.

Ariyo must look like a serious operating platform for AI employees.

---

## 1. Purpose

This skill defines the first landing page mockup for Ariyo.

The mockup must help designers, frontend engineers, founders, investors, sales teams, and product builders align around the first public website experience.

The page must communicate:

- What Ariyo is.
- Who Ariyo is for.
- Why Ariyo is different.
- How AI Employees work.
- Why enterprises can trust it.
- How Marketplace extends it.
- How Basic, Pro, and Smart fit different customers.
- What action the visitor should take next.

This file is the source of truth for landing page layout, messaging, sections, visual intent, component requirements, responsive behavior, interaction states, and handoff expectations.

## 4. Narrative Arc

The landing page should follow this sequence:

1. Ariyo is not a chatbot; it is an AI Employee Operating System.
2. Teams need AI that does real work, not disconnected AI conversations.
3. Ariyo lets companies create AI Employees with roles, skills, memory, tools, policies, and outcomes.
4. Ariyo connects AI Employees to workflows, data, integrations, and Marketplace templates.
5. Ariyo includes governance, security, approval, observability, cost control, and compliance from the beginning.
6. Ariyo measures success by business outcomes, not AI usage.
7. Customers can start simple with Basic, grow operationally with Pro, and scale intelligently with Smart.
8. The visitor should request access, book a demo, or explore the product story.

## 5. Page-Level UX Principles

### Clarity first

The user must understand what Ariyo is within 5 seconds.

### Product visible early

The hero must show the product UI or a convincing product-like operating system preview.

### Enterprise seriousness

The design must feel safe enough for enterprise buyers.

### Marketplace depth

Marketplace should be introduced as a platform flywheel, not as a side feature.

### Human + AI operating model

The page must show that humans govern AI Employees rather than losing control to them.

### Outcomes over novelty

Copy must focus on business outcomes, productivity, governance, cost control, reliability, and repeatable workflows.

## 6. Visual Direction

### Overall feel

- Premium SaaS.
- Enterprise-grade.
- Calm confidence.
- High-clarity dashboard aesthetic.
- Slightly futuristic but not sci-fi.
- Structured and trustworthy.

### Visual references to avoid

- Neon cyberpunk AI visuals.
- Floating robot mascots.
- Generic chatbot bubbles as the main concept.
- Abstract glowing spheres with no product context.
- Overly playful consumer AI design.

### Recommended design language

- Clean white / near-black / neutral foundation.
- Deep blue, slate, or indigo accents.
- Soft gradient highlights for AI intelligence.
- Cards, panels, dashboards, timelines, workflow nodes.
- Subtle motion for activation, handoff, approval, and outcome loops.
- Strong typography hierarchy.
- Enterprise-friendly trust badges.

## 24. Responsive Behavior

### Desktop

- Max content width: approximately 1200–1280px.
- Hero split layout.
- Multi-column card grids.
- Sticky nav.

### Tablet

- Hero can become stacked or 60/40.
- Cards move to 2-column layouts.
- Product mockups should remain readable.

### Mobile

- Single-column content.
- Hero product mockup simplified.
- Avoid horizontal overflow.
- CTAs stacked.
- Header becomes drawer.
- Marketplace cards become carousel or stacked list.
- Trust badges wrap cleanly.

---

## 25. Motion Guidelines

Motion must be subtle and product-oriented.

Allowed motion:

- Hero dashboard cards gently update.
- Workflow line animates through steps.
- Marketplace cards filter softly.
- Governance policy status changes on scroll.
- Outcome metric counters animate once.

Forbidden motion:

- Distracting AI particles.
- Excessive parallax.
- Constant looping animations that harm readability.
- Motion that hides critical content.

Respect reduced-motion settings.

## 34. Forbidden Patterns

The landing page must not:

- Look like a chatbot landing page.
- Claim verified certifications without approval.
- Promise full automation without governance.
- Suggest AI Employees replace all human workers.
- Hide limitations.
- Use fake customer logos.
- Use fake traction without labeling it as illustrative.
- Overload the hero with too many messages.
- Use vague AI buzzwords instead of product explanation.
- Present Marketplace as a random template gallery.
- Present Smart as uncontrolled unlimited AI.
- Ignore Basic/Pro/Smart distinction.
- Ignore enterprise buyers.
- Ignore creators and partners entirely.
- Use mock data without clarifying it is illustrative.

## 35. Success Criteria

The landing page mockup is successful when:

- A first-time visitor understands Ariyo in 5 seconds.
- The product feels larger than a chatbot.
- The AI Employee concept is clear.
- The Marketplace feels strategic.
- Enterprise governance feels credible.
- Basic / Pro / Smart feel differentiated.
- The page can support investor and enterprise conversations.
- The design can be converted into a frontend implementation plan.
- The CTA path is obvious.
- The page does not overclaim trust, compliance, or traction.
